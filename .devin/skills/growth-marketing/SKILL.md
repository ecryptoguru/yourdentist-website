# Growth Marketing

> Principles for sustainable growth through experimentation, not random tactics.
> **Measure business outcomes, not vanity metrics.**

---

## 1. Core Principles

- Start from a North Star metric and the input metrics that drive it
- Every experiment needs a hypothesis, success metric, time window, and owner
- Acquisition without activation and retention is usually waste
- Channel strategy should follow audience behavior, not trend chasing
- Unit economics must stay visible while growth accelerates

---

## 2. Funnel Thinking

Map the funnel explicitly:

- acquisition
- activation
- engagement
- retention
- referral
- monetization

Optimize the most constrained stage first.

---

## 3. Experiment Design

Each experiment should define:

- the user segment
- the bottleneck being targeted
- expected impact
- measurement window
- guardrail metrics
- what happens if the result is neutral or negative

Do not run experiments without a stop condition.

---

## 4. Key Business Metrics

Monitor:

- CAC
- LTV
- payback period
- activation rate
- retention by cohort
- experiment velocity
- winner rate
- viral coefficient when referral mechanics exist

---

## 5. Product-Led Growth Principles

- Reduce time-to-value for first session
- Remove friction from onboarding before scaling acquisition
- Design loops that encourage repeated use before referral prompts
- Align lifecycle messaging to user maturity, not calendar time alone

---

## 6. Channel Strategy

Use channels based on:

- audience presence
- creative fit
- measurable attribution confidence
- operational capacity to run and learn fast

Avoid copying the same creative across channels without adaptation.

---

## 7. Decision Checklist

Before recommending a plan:

- [ ] North Star metric defined?
- [ ] Funnel bottleneck identified?
- [ ] Hypothesis and measurement plan defined?
- [ ] Guardrail metrics defined?
- [ ] CAC/LTV implications considered?
- [ ] Retention impact considered, not just acquisition?

---

## 8. Anti-Patterns

- Declaring success from impressions alone
- Running too many experiments without learning loops
- Scaling channels before activation works
- Confusing engagement spikes with durable retention
- Ignoring economics while chasing top-line growth

---

> **Remember:** Growth is a system of repeated learning. The goal is not more activity, but more compounding wins.

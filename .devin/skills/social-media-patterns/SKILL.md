# Social Media Patterns

> Principles for platform-native social strategy that builds trust, authority, and measurable outcomes.
> **Adapt the message to the platform. Do not cross-post lazily.**

---

## 1. Core Principles

- Every platform has its own culture, incentives, and native content grammar
- Engagement quality matters more than follower count alone
- Real-time responsiveness is part of the strategy on fast platforms
- Community trust is earned through repeated value, not campaign bursts
- Promotional content should be the minority, not the baseline

---

## 2. Cross-Platform Strategy

Use a shared strategic theme, then adapt per platform:

- X: speed, commentary, threads, real-time conversation
- LinkedIn: authority, defensible opinions, professional proof, native documents
- Reddit: relationship-first, value-first, anti-spam, community norms
- Paid social: native creative, funnel segmentation, platform-specific audiences

---

## 3. Platform-Specific Rules

### X

- Respond quickly to mentions and reputation events
- Prefer insight, commentary, and conversation over pure broadcasting
- Use threads when depth helps understanding, not to fake authority

### LinkedIn

- Lead with a strong first-line hook
- Avoid external links in the post body
- Be present in the first hour after publishing
- Use specific, defensible points of view

### Reddit

- Follow the 90/10 rule: value first, promotion rare
- Learn each subreddit's local rules before posting
- Help individuals, do not mass broadcast brand copy

### Paid social

- Build creative for platform context, not one master asset for all placements
- Separate prospecting, engagement, and retargeting logic
- Validate performance against quality and downstream conversion signals

---

## 4. Measurement

Track at minimum:

- engagement quality
- response time where relevant
- share/save/comment depth
- audience growth quality
- lead or traffic contribution
- content-to-conversion signal, not just top-of-funnel vanity metrics

---

## 5. Content System Design

- Build recurring content pillars
- Reuse ideas, not identical copy
- Align cadence to operational capacity
- Use repurposing with adaptation, not duplication
- Pair editorial planning with real-time response capacity

---

## 6. Decision Checklist

Before proposing a strategy:

- [ ] Which platform-specific behaviors matter here?
- [ ] Is the content native to each platform?
- [ ] Are response-time expectations defined?
- [ ] Is the plan community-safe for Reddit or high-speed enough for X?
- [ ] Is there a measurable business outcome beyond reach?

---

## 7. Anti-Patterns

- Posting identical content everywhere
- Treating social as a one-way broadcast channel
- Ignoring community norms for speed
- Measuring success only by impressions or follower totals
- Running paid and organic as unrelated systems

---

> **Remember:** Strong social strategy is not louder content. It is better adaptation, faster learning, and more trust per interaction.

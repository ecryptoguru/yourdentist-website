## Common causes of bad breath

### Poor oral hygiene

When you do not brush and floss daily, food particles remain in your mouth. Bacteria break them down and release foul-smelling gases.

### Gum disease

Bacteria in gum pockets produce sulphur compounds that cause persistent bad breath. Gum disease is one of the leading causes of chronic halitosis.

### Dry mouth

Saliva helps wash away bacteria and food particles. Dry mouth allows bacteria to multiply, causing bad breath. Causes include dehydration, medications, mouth breathing, and sleeping.

### Food and drinks

Strong-smelling foods like garlic, onion, and spices can cause temporary bad breath. Coffee and alcohol can also dry the mouth and worsen odour.

### Smoking and tobacco

Tobacco leaves a strong smell in the mouth and also increases gum disease and dry mouth.

### Tongue coating

Bacteria and dead cells can build up on the tongue, especially at the back. This is a major cause of bad breath.

### Cavities and infections

Tooth decay, dental abscesses, and infected wounds can produce unpleasant odours.

### Medical conditions

Some health issues can cause bad breath, including:

- Sinus infections
- Tonsil stones
- Acid reflux
- Diabetes
- Liver or kidney disease
- Respiratory infections

## How to check if you have bad breath

- Lick your wrist, let it dry, and smell it
- Floss between back teeth and smell the floss
- Ask a trusted friend or family member
- Cup your hands over your mouth and nose, exhale, and smell

## Home remedies for bad breath

### Brush twice daily

Brush your teeth for two minutes, twice a day. Use a soft-bristle brush and fluoride toothpaste.

### Floss daily

Floss removes food particles and plaque from between teeth where brushing cannot reach.

### Clean your tongue

Use a tongue scraper or the back of your brush to clean your tongue every day, especially the back portion.

### Stay hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep your mouth moist and wash away bacteria.

### Chew sugar-free gum

Chewing gum stimulates saliva flow, which helps clean the mouth. Choose xylitol gum for extra benefit.

### Avoid tobacco

Quitting smoking and tobacco use improves breath and overall oral health.

### Limit strong-smelling foods

Reduce garlic, onion, and strong spices before important social events.

### Use mouthwash

Antibacterial mouthwash can temporarily reduce bad breath. However, it does not replace brushing and flossing.

## When to see a dentist for bad breath

See your dentist if:

- Bad breath persists despite good oral hygiene
- You have bleeding or swollen gums
- You have persistent dry mouth
- You notice a bad taste in your mouth
- You have tooth pain or sensitivity
- Others comment on your breath
- Bad breath returns quickly after cleaning

## Daily routine to prevent bad breath

1. Brush teeth for 2 minutes with fluoride toothpaste
2. Floss between all teeth
3. Clean tongue with scraper or brush
4. Rinse with water or antibacterial mouthwash
5. Drink water throughout the day
6. Chew sugar-free gum after meals
7. Avoid tobacco products

Following this routine consistently prevents most cases of bad breath.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why do I have bad breath even after brushing?**
Bad breath can come from between teeth, the tongue, gum disease, or medical conditions. Brushing alone may not reach these areas.

**Can gum disease cause bad breath?**
Yes, gum disease is a common cause of chronic bad breath.

**How can I get rid of bad breath permanently?**
Treat the underlying cause, which may include professional cleaning, gum treatment, cavity treatment, or managing dry mouth.

**Does mouthwash cure bad breath?**
Mouthwash masks bad breath temporarily. It does not cure the underlying cause.

**When should I see a dentist for bad breath?**
If bad breath persists for more than 2 to 3 weeks despite good oral hygiene, see a dentist.

**Can tonsil stones cause bad breath?**
Yes, tonsil stones are a common cause of persistent bad breath. If you have recurring tonsil stones, consult an ENT specialist.

**Does fasting cause bad breath?**
Yes, fasting or low-carbohydrate diets can cause bad breath (ketosis). This is temporary and resolves after eating.

**Can medications cause bad breath?**
Yes, many medications cause dry mouth, which leads to bad breath. If you suspect your medication is causing bad breath, discuss alternatives with your doctor.

**Is bad breath hereditary?**
No, bad breath is not hereditary. However, some genetic conditions can affect oral bacteria balance. Good oral hygiene prevents bad breath regardless of genetics.

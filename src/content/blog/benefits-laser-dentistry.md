## What is laser dentistry?

Laser dentistry uses focused beams of light energy to treat hard and soft tissues in the mouth. The laser can cut, reshape, clean, or disinfect tissue with minimal heat, vibration, or pressure. Different wavelengths are used for gums, teeth, and bone.

Lasers have been used in dentistry since the 1990s, and the technology has become more reliable and affordable over time. At YourDentist, we use laser-assisted systems for procedures where it genuinely improves outcomes.

## Benefit 1: Less pain and less need for anesthesia

Many laser procedures cause little or no pain because the laser seals nerve endings and blood vessels as it works. For soft tissue work like gum contouring, patients often need no anesthesia at all. For deeper procedures, anesthesia is still used, but the recovery is gentler.

This is especially helpful for patients who are afraid of injections or who have had painful dental experiences in the past.

## Benefit 2: Faster healing and less bleeding

Lasers cauterize tissue as they cut, which means less bleeding during the procedure and faster healing afterward. Swelling is usually reduced compared to traditional surgery. Gum procedures that once took a week to recover from may feel normal in two to three days.

## Benefit 3: More precise treatment

A dental laser targets only the affected tissue. Healthy surrounding tissue is left untouched. This precision is useful for gum contouring, removing decay near the nerve, and cleaning root canals in narrow spaces.

## Benefit 4: Reduced risk of infection

The high energy of the laser kills bacteria as it works. In root canal treatment, laser-assisted disinfection reaches into tiny canal branches that mechanical files can miss. In gum disease treatment, lasers reduce the bacterial load in periodontal pockets.

## Benefit 5: Less vibration and noise

Dental drills produce vibration, pressure, and a high-pitched sound that many patients find stressful. Lasers do not drill mechanically. They work silently and without vibration. This makes treatment calmer for anxious patients.

## Benefit 6: Fewer stitches and less swelling

Because lasers seal tissue as they cut, many soft tissue procedures do not need stitches. This reduces post-operative swelling and discomfort. It also lowers the risk of infection because the wound is sealed immediately.

## Benefit 7: Faster appointments

Laser-assisted procedures can often be completed more quickly than traditional methods. Single-sitting root canals, gum contouring, and teeth whitening can all be done in shorter appointments. This saves time for patients who visit us from KIIT, Patia, Saheed Nagar, and other parts of Bhubaneswar.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is laser dentistry painful?**
Most laser procedures cause minimal pain. For soft tissue work, anesthesia is often not needed. For deeper treatments, anesthesia keeps you comfortable.

**What are the disadvantages of laser dentistry?**
Lasers cannot be used for every procedure. They also cost more than traditional methods and require specialized training.

**How long does laser gum contouring take?**
A simple gum contouring procedure usually takes 30 to 60 minutes. Recovery is usually two to three days.

**Is laser dentistry safe for children?**
Yes, in many cases. Lasers are often well tolerated by children because they cause less vibration and noise.

**Does laser teeth whitening really work?**
Yes. Laser or LED activation speeds up the whitening process. Most patients see results four to eight shades lighter in one session.

**How much does laser root canal cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Laser-assisted root canal treatment at YourDentist ranges from ₹6,000 to ₹10,000 depending on the tooth and complexity.

**Can laser dentistry replace the drill entirely?**
Not entirely. Lasers are excellent for soft tissue procedures and disinfection, but some hard tissue work still requires rotary instruments. Dr. Dash uses a combination of laser and traditional tools for the best results.

**Is laser dentistry covered by insurance?**
Dental insurance in India typically does not cover laser procedures separately. However, if the laser is used as part of a covered procedure like root canal treatment, the overall procedure may be eligible for reimbursement. Check with your provider.

**How do I know if I am a candidate for laser dentistry?**
Most patients are candidates for some form of laser dentistry. Dr. Dash will assess your specific needs during consultation and recommend laser treatment where it provides clear benefits over traditional methods.

## What makes a root canal treatment the "best"?

The best root canal treatment is not just about removing infection. It is about:

- Accurate diagnosis
- Thorough cleaning of all canals
- Effective disinfection
- Proper filling and sealing
- A crown that protects the tooth
- Minimal discomfort during and after the procedure
- Long-term success without re-infection

If any step is done poorly, the tooth can fail and need re-treatment or extraction.

## How YourDentist performs root canal treatment

Dr. Arpita Dash follows a precise protocol to give every root canal the best chance of success.

### Step 1: Digital X-rays and diagnosis

We start with digital X-rays to understand the tooth's root structure. Some teeth have extra canals or curved roots that need special attention.

### Step 2: Local anesthesia

Modern anesthetic techniques numb the tooth completely. Most patients feel only mild pressure during the procedure.

### Step 3: Rubber dam isolation

A rubber dam keeps the tooth dry and free from saliva. It also protects the patient's tongue and soft tissues.

### Step 4: Pulp removal with rotary files

Rotary nickel-titanium files clean and shape the canals more efficiently than hand files. They are flexible and cause less pressure inside the tooth.

### Step 5: Laser-assisted disinfection

We use laser energy to disinfect the canals after cleaning. This reaches areas that mechanical files may miss and reduces the risk of re-infection.

### Step 6: Filling and sealing

The clean canals are filled with gutta-percha and sealed. This prevents bacteria from re-entering.

### Step 7: Crown placement

A crown protects the weakened tooth and restores normal function. We offer ceramic, zirconia, and porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns.

## Single-sitting root canal at YourDentist

Most root canals at our clinic are completed in a single sitting. This is convenient for patients and reduces the risk of contamination between visits. Single-sitting RCT is possible when the infection is localised and the canals can be cleaned thoroughly in one session.

## Aftercare for root canal treatment

After your root canal:

- Avoid chewing on the treated side until the permanent crown is placed.
- Eat soft foods for the first day.
- Take pain medication if needed for mild soreness.
- Brush gently around the area.
- Attend your follow-up appointment for crown placement.

## How to prevent needing another root canal

- Brush twice daily and floss.
- Visit the dentist every six months.
- Treat cavities before they reach the pulp.
- Avoid chewing hard objects that can crack teeth.
- Wear a mouthguard during sports.

Dental implants need a strong, healthy jawbone for support. When bone has been lost due to tooth extraction, gum disease, or injury, bone grafting may be needed before implant placement. Bone grafting rebuilds the jawbone so it can hold an implant securely. Studies show that up to 50% of bone width can be lost within the first year after tooth extraction if no grafting is performed.

In Bhubaneswar, many patients who have delayed implant treatment after tooth extraction require bone grafting because the jawbone has shrunk significantly. Early intervention with socket preservation at the time of extraction can often prevent the need for extensive grafting later.

## Why bone grafting is needed for implants

After a tooth is lost or extracted, the jawbone in that area begins to shrink. This happens because the bone no longer receives stimulation from the tooth root. Over time, the bone becomes too thin or too short to support an implant.

Bone grafting adds bone material to the area, rebuilding the height and width needed for implant placement.

## When is bone grafting needed?

Bone grafting may be recommended if:

- You have had a tooth missing for several months or years
- The jawbone has shrunk after extraction
- You have gum disease that has damaged the bone
- You have had trauma or injury to the jaw
- The sinus cavity has expanded into the upper jaw bone (needs sinus lift)
- The implant site needs more bone for stability

## Types of bone grafts

| Type | Source | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Autograft | Patient's own bone | Taken from jaw or hip. Best quality but needs second surgery site. |
| Allograft | Human donor bone | Processed donor bone. No second surgery needed. |
| Xenograft | Animal bone (usually bovine) | Treated animal bone that acts as a scaffold. |
| Synthetic | Lab-made material | Calcium-based materials that promote bone growth. |

Most grafting at YourDentist uses allograft, xenograft, or synthetic materials, which avoid the need for a second surgical site.

## What is a sinus lift?

A sinus lift is a specific type of bone grafting for the upper jaw. The maxillary sinus sits above the upper back teeth. When teeth are lost, the sinus can expand downward, leaving insufficient bone for implants.

During a sinus lift, bone material is added between the sinus floor and the jawbone. This raises the sinus and creates enough bone height for implants.

## The bone grafting procedure

### Step 1: Assessment

Dr. Dash reviews the CBCT scan to measure bone height, width, and density. The need for grafting is determined.

### Step 2: Grafting surgery

Under local anesthesia:

1. The gum is opened to expose the bone.
2. Bone graft material is placed at the site.
3. A membrane may be placed over the graft to protect it.
4. The gum is stitched closed.

The surgery takes 30 to 60 minutes for a single site.

### Step 3: Healing

The graft needs 3 to 6 months to integrate with your natural bone. During this time, new bone forms around the graft material.

### Step 4: Implant placement

Once the graft has healed, the CBCT scan is repeated to confirm bone density. The implant is then placed normally.

## Can implants be placed without grafting?

Yes, in many cases. If the bone is healthy and thick enough, implants can be placed directly. Dr. Dash determines this from the CBCT scan. About 60% to 70% of implant cases at YourDentist do not need grafting.

## What happens if you skip grafting when it is needed?

Placing an implant in insufficient bone can lead to:

- Implant failure
- Implant exposure
- Poor aesthetic result
- Nerve or sinus damage
- Infection

Grafting when recommended improves the long-term success of the implant.

## What to eat after bone grafting

- Soft, cool foods for the first 3 days
- Avoid hot, spicy, or crunchy foods
- Do not chew on the graft side for 1 to 2 weeks
- Gradually return to normal diet after 2 weeks

## Caring for the graft site

- Take prescribed antibiotics and pain medication
- Do not rinse vigorously for 48 hours
- Avoid smoking, which slows healing
- Do not touch the site with tongue or fingers
- Sleep with head slightly elevated for the first few nights

## Bone grafting success factors

| Factor | Why it matters | How to optimise |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Smoking | Reduces blood flow and healing | Quit at least 2 weeks before and after |
| Oral hygiene | Prevents infection at graft site | Gentle cleaning, prescribed rinses |
| Diet | Nutrition supports bone regeneration | Calcium-rich soft foods, protein |
| Medical conditions | Diabetes slows healing | Control blood sugar before surgery |
| Graft material | Quality affects integration | Dr. Dash selects best material for each case |
| Healing time | Rushing can cause graft failure | Wait full 3 to 6 months before implant |

Following these guidelines gives your bone graft the best chance of successful integration.

## Socket preservation vs delayed grafting

| Aspect | Socket preservation | Delayed grafting |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Timing | At time of extraction | Months or years after extraction |
| Bone loss | Prevented | Already occurred |
| Procedure complexity | Simpler | More complex |
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Healing time | 3 to 4 months | 4 to 6 months |
| Implant success | Higher | Good, but slightly lower |

Socket preservation at the time of extraction is the best way to maintain bone for future implants. Dr. Dash recommends this whenever possible.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is bone grafting painful?**
The procedure is done under anesthesia. Most patients feel mild discomfort for 2 to 3 days after, manageable with pain medication.

**How long does bone grafting take to heal?**
Bone grafts take 3 to 6 months to integrate before an implant can be placed.

**Can I get an implant without bone grafting?**
Yes, if your jawbone is thick and dense enough. Dr. Dash will determine this from your CBCT scan.

**How much does bone grafting cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Bone grafting costs ₹5,000 to ₹30,000 depending on the type and extent of grafting needed.

**What is a sinus lift?**
A sinus lift adds bone to the upper jaw near the sinus cavity to create enough height for dental implants.

**Can smokers get bone grafts?**
Smoking significantly reduces graft success rates. Dr. Dash strongly recommends quitting smoking before grafting and during the healing period. Patients who continue smoking have a higher risk of graft failure.

**How do I know if I need bone grafting?**
A CBCT scan at YourDentist will show the exact bone height, width, and density. If the bone is insufficient for implant placement, Dr. Dash will recommend grafting.

**Is bone grafting safe?**
Yes, bone grafting is a well-established procedure with a high success rate. The materials used are processed and sterilised to eliminate any risk of disease transmission. Dr. Dash uses only certified grafting materials.

**Can bone grafting and implant placement be done at the same time?**
In some cases, minor grafting can be done simultaneously with implant placement. This reduces the total treatment time. However, for larger grafts or sinus lifts, a separate healing period is needed before implant placement.

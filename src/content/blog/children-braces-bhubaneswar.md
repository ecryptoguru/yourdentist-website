## When should children get braces?

Children should have their first orthodontic evaluation by age 7. At this age, permanent teeth are starting to come in, and jaw growth can be guided. Most children get braces between ages 10 and 14, when most permanent teeth have erupted.

## Signs your child may need braces

- Crowded or overlapping teeth
- Gaps between teeth
- Early or late loss of baby teeth
- Difficulty chewing or biting
- Biting the cheek or roof of the mouth
- Teeth that do not meet properly
- Jaw shifts or clicking sounds
- Thumb sucking beyond age 4
- Mouth breathing

## Early orthodontic treatment

Some children need early treatment, called Phase 1 orthodontics, around age 7 to 10. This may include:

- **Expanders to widen the jaw:** A narrow upper jaw can cause crossbite and crowding. An expander widens the jaw gradually, creating space for permanent teeth.
- **Space maintainers after early tooth loss:** If a baby tooth is lost too early, neighbouring teeth can shift into the space. A space maintainer keeps the space open for the permanent tooth.
- **Habit-breaking appliances for thumb sucking:** These small devices discourage thumb sucking and prevent dental changes.
- **Partial braces for specific problems:** Sometimes only a few teeth need correction early on.

Early treatment can reduce the need for more extensive treatment later. It can also prevent problems that would require surgery in adulthood.

## The braces process for children

### Step 1: Consultation

Dr. Dash examines your child's teeth, takes X-rays, and discusses treatment options.

### Step 2: Records

Photographs, impressions, or digital scans are taken for treatment planning.

### Step 3: Braces placement

Brackets are bonded to the teeth and connected with wires. The process is painless.

### Step 4: Adjustments

Your child visits every 4 to 8 weeks for adjustments. Wires are tightened to gradually move teeth.

### Step 5: Retainers

After braces are removed, retainers keep teeth in position. Retainers may be removable or fixed.

## How long do children wear braces?

Most children wear braces for 12 to 24 months. Complex cases may take longer. Early Phase 1 treatment may take 6 to 12 months.

## Braces care for children

- Brush after every meal
- Avoid sticky, hard, and chewy foods
- Wear a mouthguard during sports
- Attend all adjustment appointments
- Do not pick at wires or brackets
- Use orthodontic floss or water flossers

## Helping your child adjust to braces

- Explain that mild soreness is normal for the first few days
- Serve soft foods like dal, rice, curd, and mashed vegetables initially
- Use orthodontic wax on irritating brackets
- Encourage your child to follow care instructions
- Praise them for keeping their braces clean
- Remind them that the result will be worth it

## Frequently asked questions

**What is the right age for braces?**
The first orthodontic evaluation should happen by age 7. Most children get braces between ages 10 and 14.

**How much do braces cost for children in Bhubaneswar?**
Metal braces cost ₹25,000 to ₹40,000 at YourDentist.

**Are braces painful for children?**
Braces may cause mild soreness for a few days after placement and adjustments. This is normal and manageable.

**How long do children need braces?**
Most children wear braces for 12 to 24 months.

**Can my child eat normally with braces?**
Your child should avoid hard, sticky, and chewy foods. Most soft foods are fine.

**Will braces affect my child's speech?**
Braces may cause a slight lisp for the first few days, but speech typically returns to normal within a week.

**What if a bracket breaks?**
Contact YourDentist immediately. Dr. Dash will repair or replace the bracket. In the meantime, cover any sharp edges with orthodontic wax.

**Can my child play sports with braces?**
Yes, but they should wear a mouthguard designed for braces to protect both teeth and brackets.

**How often are adjustment visits needed?**
Most children need adjustment visits every 4 to 8 weeks.

**Can my child get clear aligners instead of braces?**
Clear aligners are suitable for older children and teenagers who have all permanent teeth and can follow instructions reliably. Dr. Dash will assess if your child is a good candidate for aligners during the consultation.

**What if my child does not want braces?**
Dr. Dash is experienced in working with reluctant children. She explains the process in a child-friendly way and helps them feel involved in their treatment. Ceramic braces or clear aligners can also help self-conscious teens feel more comfortable.

**Will my child need teeth extracted before braces?**
Some children need extractions to create space for crowded teeth. Dr. Dash evaluates this during the consultation and only recommends extractions when necessary. Not all children need extractions before braces.

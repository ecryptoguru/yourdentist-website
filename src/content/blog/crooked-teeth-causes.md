## Genetic causes of crooked teeth

Genetics play a major role in tooth alignment. You may inherit:

- Jaw size from one parent and tooth size from another, leading to crowding
- A small jaw that cannot fit all teeth properly
- A large jaw that creates gaps
- Protruding front teeth
- A deep bite or open bite

If your parents needed braces, there is a higher chance you or your children will too.

## Childhood habits that cause crooked teeth

Certain habits during childhood can push teeth out of position:

### Thumb sucking

Sucking the thumb after age 5 can push the front teeth forward and create an open bite. It can also narrow the upper jaw.

### Prolonged pacifier use

Using a pacifier beyond age 3 can affect jaw development and tooth position.

### Tongue thrusting

Pushing the tongue against the front teeth when swallowing or speaking can move teeth forward over time.

### Mouth breathing

Breathing through the mouth instead of the nose can change the shape of the jaw and affect tooth alignment.

### Nail biting

Biting nails puts pressure on teeth and can cause them to shift or chip.

## Tooth loss and crooked teeth

Losing a tooth, especially a baby tooth too early, can cause neighbouring teeth to shift into the gap. This leaves less space for the permanent tooth to come in. Adult tooth loss also leads to shifting and can cause bite problems.

## Other causes of crooked teeth

| Cause | How it affects teeth |
| --- | --- |
| Early loss of baby teeth | Neighbouring teeth shift, blocking permanent teeth |
| Late loss of baby teeth | Permanent teeth erupt in wrong positions |
| Extra teeth | Crowding and misalignment |
| Missing teeth | Teeth shift into gaps |
| Jaw injury | Fractures or uneven growth affect alignment |
| Tumours or cysts | Pressure on teeth can move them |
| Poor dental restorations | Badly shaped crowns can affect bite |

## Problems caused by crooked teeth

Crooked teeth are not just a cosmetic issue. They can cause:

- Difficulty cleaning, leading to cavities and gum disease
- Uneven wear on tooth surfaces
- Jaw pain and TMJ problems
- Difficulty chewing
- Speech problems
- Low self-confidence

## Treatment options for crooked teeth

### Metal braces

Metal braces are the most common and effective treatment for crooked teeth. They work for all levels of crowding and bite problems.

### Ceramic braces

Ceramic braces are less visible than metal and work similarly. They are a good choice for image-conscious patients.

### Clear aligners

Clear aligners are removable and nearly invisible. They work well for mild to moderate crowding.

### Self-ligating braces

Self-ligating braces use a sliding mechanism instead of elastic ties. They may reduce friction and appointment frequency.

### Retainers

After active treatment, retainers keep teeth in position.

## How to fix crooked teeth without braces

For very minor crookedness, options like dental bonding or veneers can improve appearance. However, these do not actually move teeth. For proper alignment, braces or aligners are usually needed.

| Gum disease | Plaque retention in crowded areas | Bone loss and tooth mobility |

Addressing crooked teeth early prevents these long-term consequences and improves both oral health and quality of life.

## Can crooked teeth be prevented?

Some causes cannot be prevented, such as genetics. But parents can help by:

- Stopping thumb sucking by age 5
- Limiting pacifier use after age 3
- Treating mouth breathing
- Treating tongue thrust
- Taking children for early orthodontic evaluation
- Using space maintainers if baby teeth are lost early

## Frequently asked questions

**Why are my teeth crooked?**
Crooked teeth can be caused by genetics, childhood habits, tooth loss, jaw size, or injury.

**Can crooked teeth cause health problems?**
Yes, crooked teeth can make cleaning difficult, leading to cavities, gum disease, and jaw problems.

**What is the best treatment for crooked teeth?**
Braces or clear aligners are the most effective treatments. The best option depends on the severity and your preference.

**Can crooked teeth be fixed without braces?**
Minor cosmetic issues can be improved with veneers, but true alignment usually requires braces or aligners.

**At what age should crooked teeth be treated?**
Early evaluation by age 7 helps plan treatment. Full braces usually happen between ages 10 and 14.

**How much do braces cost for crooked teeth in Bhubaneswar?**
Metal braces cost ₹25,000 to ₹40,000, and clear aligners cost ₹60,000 to ₹1,50,000.

**Can crooked teeth get worse with age?**
Yes, teeth can continue to shift throughout life, especially if retainers are not worn after orthodontic treatment. Wisdom teeth eruption can also cause crowding in the lower front teeth.

**Are clear aligners effective for severely crooked teeth?**
Clear aligners work well for mild to moderate crowding. For severe cases, traditional braces may be more effective. Dr. Dash will assess your case and recommend the best option.

**Can I fix crooked teeth if I am over 40?**
Yes, there is no upper age limit for orthodontic treatment. Adult treatment may take slightly longer but results are equally effective. Many adults in Bhubaneswar choose clear aligners for discreet treatment.

**Will I need extractions before braces?**
Extractions may be needed if there is severe crowding and not enough space for all teeth. Dr. Dash will assess whether extractions are necessary or if expansion can create enough space.

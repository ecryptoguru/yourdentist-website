## How often should you visit the dentist?

Most people should visit the dentist every 6 months. Some people may need more frequent visits, including:

- People with gum disease
- People with diabetes
- Smokers
- Pregnant women
- People with a high risk of cavities
- Children with orthodontic appliances
- People with weakened immune systems

## What happens during a dental checkup?

### Medical history review

Dr. Dash asks about any health conditions, medications, and dental concerns.

### Visual examination

Teeth, gums, tongue, cheeks, and throat are checked for signs of disease, decay, or oral cancer.

### Gum assessment

The depth of gum pockets is measured to check for gum disease.

### X-rays

X-rays may be taken to detect cavities between teeth, bone loss, impacted teeth, or other hidden problems.

### Cleaning

Plaque and tartar are removed through scaling and polishing.

### Treatment planning

If any problems are found, Dr. Dash explains the diagnosis and treatment options.

## Benefits of regular dental checkups

### Early detection of cavities

Cavities caught early need simple fillings. Untreated cavities may need root canals or extractions.

### Gum disease prevention

Regular cleaning and monitoring prevent gingivitis from becoming periodontitis.

### Oral cancer screening

Dentists check for early signs of oral cancer, which is more treatable when detected early.

### Save money

Preventive care costs much less than treating advanced dental problems.

### Maintain a bright smile

Polishing removes surface stains and keeps teeth looking clean.

### Protect overall health

Oral health is connected to heart disease, diabetes, and other conditions.

## Signs you should visit the dentist sooner

- Tooth pain or sensitivity
- Bleeding gums
- Persistent bad breath
- Swelling in the mouth or face
- Loose teeth
- Mouth sores that do not heal
- Jaw pain or clicking
- Difficulty chewing

## Regular checkups for children

Children should visit the dentist every 6 months starting from their first birthday. Early visits help:

- Monitor tooth development
- Prevent cavities
- Build comfort with dental visits
- Teach good oral habits
- Apply fluoride and sealants

## Regular checkups for older adults

Older adults may need more frequent dental visits because of:

- Dry mouth from medications
- Gum recession
- Wear on teeth
- Denture-related issues
- Higher risk of oral cancer

## Why skip dental visits can cost more

A small cavity left untreated can become a root canal. Gum disease left untreated can lead to tooth loss. Regular visits catch problems before they become expensive.

## The connection between oral health and overall health

Your mouth is a window into your overall health. During a dental checkup, Dr. Dash may detect signs of:

- **Diabetes:** Gum disease that is hard to control can be a sign of diabetes.
- **Heart disease:** Gum inflammation is linked to cardiovascular problems.
- **Osteoporosis:** Bone loss in the jaw may indicate osteoporosis.
- **Acid reflux:** Eroded enamel can be a sign of chronic acid reflux.
- **Vitamin deficiencies:** Mouth ulcers and tongue changes can indicate nutritional deficiencies.
- **Stress:** Teeth grinding and jaw pain are often stress-related.

## Frequently asked questions

**How often should I visit the dentist?**
Most people should visit every 6 months. Some people with gum disease or other risk factors may need more frequent visits.

**What happens if I do not visit the dentist regularly?**
Small problems can become serious and expensive to treat. Gum disease and cavities may progress unnoticed.

**Is a dental checkup painful?**
No, a routine checkup and cleaning is usually painless. Deep cleaning for gum disease may cause mild discomfort.

**How much does a dental checkup cost in Bhubaneswar?**
A checkup with X-rays and cleaning costs ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 at YourDentist.

**Do I need X-rays every visit?**
No. X-rays are usually taken once a year or when specific problems are suspected.

**Can I eat before a dental checkup?**
Yes, but try to brush your teeth before coming. Avoid staining foods like coffee just before your appointment.

**How long does a dental checkup take?**
A routine checkup with cleaning takes 30 to 45 minutes. A comprehensive checkup with X-rays may take 60 minutes.

**What if I am anxious about visiting the dentist?**
Dr. Dash is experienced in helping anxious patients. Let our team know about your anxiety so we can make your visit as comfortable as possible.

**Can dental checkups detect oral cancer?**
Yes. Oral cancer screening is part of every comprehensive checkup at YourDentist. Dr. Dash examines your lips, tongue, cheeks, throat, and palate for any suspicious changes. Early detection of oral cancer dramatically improves treatment outcomes.

**Should I bring my child for checkups even if they have no complaints?**
Yes. Children should start dental visits by age 1 and continue every 6 months. Many dental problems in children are silent and only detected by a dentist. Regular visits also build comfort and positive dental habits.

**What if I have not been to a dentist in years?**
It is never too late to start. Dr. Dash will perform a comprehensive assessment, identify any existing problems, and create a treatment plan. Many patients who have avoided the dentist for years are relieved to find that modern dental care is comfortable and judgment-free.

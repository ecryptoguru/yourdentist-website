## Why dental costs differ between cities

Several factors affect dental treatment pricing:

- **Clinic rent and overhead:** Prime locations in metro cities charge high rent, which adds to treatment costs.
- **Staff salaries:** Metro cities have higher salary expectations for dentists and assistants.
- **Technology and equipment:** Imported equipment and materials cost more, especially in high-end metro clinics.
- **Dentist experience:** Highly reputed specialists in metros charge premium fees.
- **Local competition:** More clinics in a city can sometimes lower prices, but premium areas remain expensive.
- **Demand and purchasing power:** Cities with higher incomes can support higher prices.

## How to compare costs across cities

When comparing costs, look at the total cost of care, not just one procedure. Include:

- Consultation and X-rays
- Main procedure
- Materials and crown cost
- Medications
- Follow-up visits
- Travel and accommodation if coming from another city
- Time away from work

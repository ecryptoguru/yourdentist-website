Dental implants have a success rate of 95% to 98%, but failures can happen. Implant failure means the implant does not fuse with the bone, becomes loose, or develops an infection. Recognising the signs early improves the chances of saving the implant. Research shows that early implant failure occurs in about 1% to 5% of cases, while late failure affects about 5% to 10% of implants over 10 years.

In Bhubaneswar, many implant failures are preventable and result from poor oral hygiene, smoking, or skipping follow-up visits. Understanding the signs of implant failure and seeking prompt treatment can often save the implant or allow successful replacement.

## Signs of dental implant failure

Contact your dentist immediately if you notice:

- Pain or discomfort around the implant
- The implant feels loose or moves
- Swelling or redness in the gum around the implant
- Pus or bleeding from the implant site
- Bad taste or smell that does not go away
- Gum recession exposing the implant threads
- Difficulty chewing on the implant side

## Common causes of implant failure

### Poor osseointegration

The implant may not fuse properly with the bone. This can happen due to low bone density, movement of the implant during healing, or infection.

### Smoking

Smoking reduces blood flow to the gums and bone, slowing healing and increasing the risk of failure. Smokers have a failure rate about twice that of non-smokers.

### Infection (peri-implantitis)

Bacteria can infect the tissue around the implant, causing inflammation and bone loss. This is similar to gum disease but around an implant.

### Poor oral hygiene

Not brushing and flossing around the implant allows plaque to build up, leading to infection and bone loss.

### Medical conditions

Uncontrolled diabetes, osteoporosis, and autoimmune diseases can affect healing and implant success.

### Implant overload

Grinding teeth or placing too much force on the implant can cause bone loss and failure. A nightguard can protect implants in patients with bruxism.

### Poor surgical technique

Incorrect implant placement, insufficient bone, or contamination during surgery can lead to failure. This is why choosing an experienced dentist matters.

### Delayed crown placement

Waiting too long to place the crown after healing can sometimes cause problems with the gum and implant interface.

## How is implant failure diagnosed?

Dr. Dash diagnoses implant failure through:

- Clinical examination of the implant and surrounding gum
- X-rays or CBCT scan to check bone levels
- Testing implant mobility
- Checking for signs of infection

## Treatment options for failed implants

### Early failure

If the implant has not fused with the bone, it is removed. The area is cleaned and allowed to heal. After 2 to 4 months, a new implant can be placed, sometimes with bone grafting.

### Late failure (peri-implantitis)

If the implant is still stable but there is bone loss around it, treatment may include:

- Deep cleaning around the implant
- Antibiotic therapy
- Laser disinfection
- Bone grafting around the implant
- Adjusting the bite to reduce overload

### Imant removal and replacement

If the implant cannot be saved, it is removed. After healing, a new implant can be placed. In some cases, bone grafting is needed before the new implant.

## Preventing implant failure

1. **Choose an experienced dentist** with proper training in implant surgery.
2. **Follow aftercare instructions** carefully after surgery.
3. **Do not smoke** before or after implant treatment.
4. **Maintain excellent oral hygiene** — brush twice daily and floss around implants.
5. **Visit the dentist every 6 months** for checkups and cleaning.
6. **Wear a nightguard** if you grind your teeth.
7. **Control medical conditions** like diabetes before and after implant treatment.
8. **Avoid chewing hard objects** like ice, pens, or bones.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a failed dental implant be replaced?**
Yes, in most cases. After removing the failed implant and allowing healing, a new implant can be placed, sometimes with bone grafting.

**How common is dental implant failure?**
Implant failure is rare. The success rate is 95% to 98% over 10 years.

**What are the first signs of implant failure?**
Pain, looseness, swelling, bleeding, or pus around the implant are early signs. Contact your dentist immediately.

**Can smoking cause implant failure?**
Yes, smoking significantly increases the risk of implant failure by reducing blood flow and slowing healing.

**How is peri-implantitis treated?**
Treatment includes deep cleaning, antibiotics, laser disinfection, and sometimes bone grafting around the implant.

**Can I prevent implant failure?**
Yes, most implant failures are preventable. Choose an experienced dentist, maintain excellent oral hygiene, do not smoke, wear a nightguard if you grind, and attend all follow-up appointments.

**How often should I get my implants checked?**
We recommend a professional checkup and cleaning every 6 months. Dr. Dash may recommend more frequent visits if you have risk factors like smoking or diabetes.

**Does insurance cover implant failure treatment?**
Most dental insurance plans do not cover implants or implant failure treatment. We provide detailed bills for any eligible reimbursement.

**Can peri-implantitis be cured?**
Early-stage peri-implantitis (mucositis) can be reversed with professional cleaning and improved hygiene. Advanced peri-implantitis with bone loss is harder to treat but can often be managed with laser therapy and bone grafting.

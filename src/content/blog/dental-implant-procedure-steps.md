A dental implant procedure replaces a missing tooth with a permanent, natural-looking artificial tooth. The process involves surgery, healing, and crown placement. Understanding each step helps patients feel prepared and confident. Dental implants have a success rate of 95% to 98% over 10 years, making them one of the most reliable dental procedures available.

## Step 1: Consultation and assessment

The first visit includes:

- Discussion of your dental history and concerns
- Clinical examination of the mouth
- CBCT scan to assess bone density and volume
- Digital impressions or photographs
- Review of medical history

Dr. Dash evaluates whether you are a good candidate for implants and creates a personalised treatment plan.

## Step 2: Treatment planning

Based on the assessment, Dr. Dash plans:

- The number and position of implants
- Whether bone grafting is needed
- The type of crown or bridge
- The timeline and cost

You receive a written estimate and a clear explanation of what to expect.

## Step 3: Pre-surgery preparation

Before surgery, you may need:

- Teeth cleaning or treatment of gum disease
- Extraction of any remaining tooth roots at the implant site
- Bone grafting if the jawbone is too thin (healing takes 3 to 6 months before implant placement)
- Antibiotics to prevent infection

## Step 4: Implant surgery

The surgery is done under local anesthesia. The steps are:

1. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia numbs the area. You remain awake but feel no pain.
2. **Incision:** A small cut is made in the gum to expose the bone.
3. **Drilling:** A precise hole is drilled into the jawbone at the planned angle and depth.
4. **Implant placement:** The titanium implant is inserted into the bone.
5. **Closure:** The gum is stitched closed over or around the implant.

The surgery takes 45 to 90 minutes for a single implant. For multiple implants, it may take longer.

## Step 5: Healing and osseointegration

After surgery, the implant needs time to fuse with the jawbone. This process is called osseointegration.

| Healing stage | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Initial gum healing | 1 to 2 weeks |
| Bone fusion | 2 to 6 months |
| Total healing before crown | 3 to 6 months |

During this time, you may wear a temporary denture or bridge if the missing tooth is visible.

## Step 6: Abutment placement

Once the implant has fused with the bone, a second minor procedure exposes the implant and attaches an abutment. The abutment is the connector that holds the crown.

This step takes 15 to 30 minutes and is done under local anesthesia. The gum then heals around the abutment for 1 to 2 weeks.

## Step 7: Crown impression and fabrication

After the gum heals, impressions are taken of the abutment and surrounding teeth. The crown is custom-made to match the colour, shape, and size of your natural teeth.

| Crown type | Time to fabricate |
| --- | --- |
| Metal-ceramic | 5 to 7 days |
| Zirconia | 7 to 10 days |

## Step 8: Crown placement

The final crown is attached to the abutment. Dr. Dash checks the fit, bite, and appearance. Adjustments are made if needed.

The crown is either cemented or screwed into place. Screwed crowns can be removed for maintenance if needed in the future.

## Step 9: Follow-up and maintenance

After crown placement, you visit YourDentist for a follow-up check. Regular 6-monthly checkups are recommended to monitor the implant.

## Pain and recovery after implant surgery

| Time | What to expect |
| --- | --- |
| First 24 hours | Numbness, mild swelling, some discomfort |
| 2 to 3 days | Swelling peaks, then reduces |
| 1 week | Stitches removed, most discomfort gone |
| 2 weeks | Gum healed, normal activities resumed |

Most patients manage discomfort with normal pain medication. Ice packs help reduce swelling in the first 48 hours.

## What to eat after implant surgery

### First 3 days

- Soft foods: yogurt, mashed potatoes, soup, smoothies
- Cool or room temperature foods
- Avoid hot, spicy, or crunchy foods

### Days 4 to 7

- Soft foods that require minimal chewing
- Avoid chewing on the surgery side
- Continue avoiding hard or sticky foods

### After 1 week

- Gradually return to normal diet
- Avoid very hard foods for 4 to 6 weeks

## Risks and complications

Dental implant surgery has a high success rate of 95% to 98%. Possible but rare complications include:

- Infection at the implant site
- Implant not fusing with bone
- Nerve damage (very rare with proper planning)
- Sinus problems (upper jaw implants)
- Gum recession around the implant

Dr. Dash uses CBCT-guided planning to minimise these risks.

## Implant procedure timeline summary

| Step | Time | What happens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Consultation | 1 visit | Assessment, CBCT scan, treatment plan |
| Pre-surgery | 1 to 2 visits | Cleaning, extractions if needed |
| Bone grafting (if needed) | 3 to 6 months | Graft heals before implant |
| Implant surgery | 45 to 90 minutes | Implant placed in jawbone |
| Osseointegration | 2 to 6 months | Implant fuses with bone |
| Abutment placement | 15 to 30 minutes | Connector attached |
| Crown fabrication | 5 to 10 days | Custom crown made |
| Crown placement | 1 visit | Final tooth attached |
| Follow-up | Every 6 months | Monitoring and cleaning |

The total timeline from consultation to final crown is typically 3 to 7 months, depending on whether grafting is needed.

## Frequently asked questions

**How painful is dental implant surgery?**
The surgery is done under anesthesia. Most patients feel mild discomfort for 2 to 3 days after, similar to a tooth extraction.

**How long does the implant procedure take?**
A single implant surgery takes 45 to 90 minutes. The full process including healing and crown takes 3 to 6 months.

**Can I go to work after implant surgery?**
Most patients return to work the next day. If your job is physically demanding, take 1 to 2 days off.

**How do I know if I need bone grafting?**
The CBCT scan shows your bone density. If the bone is too thin, Dr. Dash will recommend grafting before implant placement.

**Can I get a tooth the same day as implant surgery?**
In some cases, a temporary tooth can be placed immediately. This depends on bone quality and implant stability.

**How much does a dental implant cost at YourDentist?**
A single tooth implant costs ₹25,000 to ₹50,000 including the implant, surgery, abutment, and crown. Dr. Dash will provide a written estimate after your consultation.

**Can I get multiple implants in one surgery?**
Yes, multiple implants can be placed in a single session. For full mouth cases, 4 to 8 implants per arch are placed in one surgery lasting 2 to 4 hours.

**What if I am afraid of dental surgery?**
Dr. Dash uses gentle techniques and modern anesthesia to ensure comfort. Most patients report that implant surgery is easier than expected. If you are very anxious, discuss sedation options during your consultation.

**How do I care for my implant after surgery?**
Eat soft foods for the first week, avoid smoking, take prescribed medications, and keep the area clean with gentle rinsing. Dr. Dash will provide detailed aftercare instructions after surgery.

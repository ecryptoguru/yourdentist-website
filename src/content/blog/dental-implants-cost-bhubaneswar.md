## What is a dental implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium post that acts as an artificial tooth root. It is placed into the jawbone and fuses with the bone over time. After healing, a crown is attached to the implant. The result is a permanent, stable tooth replacement.

## Factors that affect dental implant cost

### Number of implants

A single missing tooth needs one implant. Multiple missing teeth may need two or more implants, possibly with a bridge. Full mouth rehabilitation can require 4 to 8 implants per arch.

### Implant brand and quality

Different implant brands have different prices. Premium brands from Europe, the USA, or South Korea often cost more than Indian or basic brands. The brand also affects long-term reliability and warranty.

### Bone grafting

If the jawbone is too thin or soft, bone grafting may be needed before implant placement. This adds to the cost and healing time.

### Type of crown

The crown placed on the implant can be metal-ceramic, all-ceramic, or zirconia. Zirconia crowns cost more but look the most natural.

### Dentist experience

An experienced implant dentist may charge more than a less experienced one. The success of implant treatment depends heavily on planning and surgical skill.

### Diagnostic tests

CBCT scans, X-rays, and digital impressions are needed for accurate planning. These are usually charged separately.

## Full mouth implant options

### All-on-4 implants

All-on-4 uses 4 implants to support a full arch of teeth. It is a cost-effective option for patients who have lost most or all teeth in one arch.

### All-on-6 implants

All-on-6 uses 6 implants for extra support. It is suitable for patients with good bone density.

### Implant-supported overdenture

An overdenture snaps onto 2 to 4 implants. It is more stable than a regular denture and less expensive than a fixed full arch.

## What is a dental implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium post that acts as an artificial tooth root. It is surgically placed into the jawbone. Over a few months, the bone fuses with the implant in a process called osseointegration. After healing, a connector called an abutment is attached, and a crown is placed on top.

The result is a permanent, stable tooth replacement that looks and functions like a natural tooth.

## When do you need a dental implant?

You may be a candidate for a dental implant if you:

- Have lost one or more teeth due to decay, injury, or gum disease
- Have a broken tooth that cannot be saved
- Wear dentures that slip or feel loose
- Want a permanent alternative to a dental bridge
- Have enough healthy jawbone to support an implant

If your bone is too thin or too soft, bone grafting may be needed before implant placement.

## The dental implant procedure step by step

### Step 1: Consultation and assessment

The first visit includes a clinical examination, digital X-rays, and a CBCT scan if needed. We check your bone quality, the position of your sinuses and nerves, and your overall oral health. We also review your medical history and medications.

### Step 2: Treatment planning

Based on the scans, we plan the exact position, angle, and depth of the implant. At YourDentist, we use digital planning to avoid guesswork. This reduces complications and improves the long-term success rate.

### Step 3: Tooth extraction if needed

If the damaged tooth is still in place, it is removed first. In some cases, the implant can be placed immediately after extraction. In other cases, we wait two to three months for the socket to heal.

### Step 4: Bone grafting if needed

If the jawbone is too thin or has shrunk after tooth loss, bone graft material is added. This can be done at the same time as implant placement or as a separate procedure. Healing after bone grafting usually takes three to six months.

### Step 5: Implant placement surgery

The implant is placed into the jawbone through a small incision in the gum. The procedure is done under local anesthesia. You should not feel pain, only pressure. Most single-tooth implant placements take 30 to 60 minutes.

After placement, the gum is closed over the implant, and a healing period of two to six months begins.

### Step 6: Healing and osseointegration

During healing, the bone grows around the implant and locks it in place. This is the most important phase. Smoking, uncontrolled diabetes, and poor oral hygiene can slow or stop this process.

### Step 7: Abutment placement

Once the implant has fused with the bone, a small connector called an abutment is attached. This minor procedure opens the gum, connects the abutment, and allows the gum to heal around it.

### Step 8: Crown placement

We take impressions or digital scans and send them to a dental lab. A custom crown is made to match your natural teeth in colour, shape, and size. The crown is then attached to the abutment. After this, you can chew normally.

## How long does the dental implant procedure take?

The total time from implant placement to final crown is usually two to six months. If bone grafting is needed, the timeline may extend to six to twelve months. The surgery itself is usually completed in one appointment, and most patients return to normal activities the next day.

## Is dental implant surgery painful?

Implant surgery is done under local anesthesia, so the procedure itself is painless. After the anesthesia wears off, you may feel mild swelling and discomfort for two to three days. This is usually managed with pain medication and cold compresses. Most patients say the discomfort is less than they expected.

## How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last 15 years or longer. In many cases, they last a lifetime. The crown on top may need replacement after 10 to 15 years due to normal wear. Good oral hygiene, regular checkups, and not smoking are the biggest factors for long-term success.

## Are dental implants safe?

Yes. Dental implants are one of the safest and most predictable procedures in dentistry. The success rate is over 95% for healthy patients. Implants are made from biocompatible titanium, which is well tolerated by the body. Complications are rare when the procedure is planned and performed by an experienced dental surgeon.

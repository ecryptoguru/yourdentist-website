## What are dentures?

Dentures are removable appliances that replace missing teeth. They sit on the gums and are held in place by suction, adhesive, or clips. Dentures can be full (replacing all teeth) or partial (replacing some teeth while attaching to remaining teeth).

## What are dental implants?

Dental implants are titanium posts that are surgically placed into the jawbone. They act as artificial roots. Crowns, bridges, or dentures can be attached to implants. Implants do not come out and feel like natural teeth.

## When dentures are the better choice

Dentures may be the better option if:

- You have significant bone loss and cannot have bone grafting
- You have health conditions that make surgery risky
- You want the lowest upfront cost
- You want a non-surgical solution
- You need teeth replaced quickly
- You are not a candidate for implants

## When dental implants are the better choice

Implants may be the better option if:

- You want a permanent, stable solution
- You want to preserve jawbone
- You want to eat and speak normally
- You are healthy enough for minor surgery
- You can invest more upfront for long-term value
- You have enough bone or are willing to have grafting

## Types of dentures

### Full dentures

Full dentures replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw. They rest on the gums and rely on suction and muscle control.

### Partial dentures

Partial dentures replace some missing teeth. They attach to remaining teeth with metal or plastic clasps.

### Implant-supported dentures

Implant-supported dentures combine the two options. Two to four implants hold a denture in place. This is more stable than regular dentures but less expensive than a fixed full-arch bridge.

## Pros and cons of dental implants

### Pros

- Look and feel like natural teeth
- Preserve jawbone
- Do not slip
- Long-lasting
- Improve chewing and speech
- No need for adhesives

### Cons

- Higher upfront cost
- Requires surgery
- Longer healing time
- Not suitable for everyone
- May need bone grafting

## Pros and cons of dentures

### Pros of dentures

- Lower upfront cost
- No surgery needed
- Can be made quickly
- Suitable for many patients
- Can replace many teeth at once

### Cons of dentures

- Can slip and cause discomfort
- Need adhesives for stability
- Do not preserve bone
- Can affect speech and taste
- Need regular replacement
- Must be removed for cleaning

## Combining implants and dentures

For patients who want better stability than dentures but cannot afford full implants, implant-supported dentures are a middle option. Two to four implants hold a removable denture firmly in place. This improves chewing and reduces slipping.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are dental implants better than dentures?**
Implants are usually better for long-term function and bone health, but dentures are a good low-cost option.

**Are dentures cheaper than implants?**
Yes, dentures have a lower upfront cost, but implants may cost less over time because they last longer.

**Can dentures be converted to implants?**
Yes, some patients start with dentures and later switch to implants or implant-supported dentures.

**Do dentures affect speech?**
Dentures can affect speech initially, but most patients adapt within a few weeks.

**Can I eat normally with dentures?**
Dentures allow most eating, but hard and sticky foods can be difficult.

**How many implants do I need for full dentures?**
Implant-supported dentures usually need 2 to 4 implants per arch.

**Can I get implants if I already wear dentures?**
Yes, many patients switch from dentures to implants or implant-supported dentures. Dr. Dash will assess your bone health with a CBCT scan to determine if you are a candidate.

**How long do dentures last before needing replacement?**
Dentures typically last 5 to 8 years. Over time, the jawbone shrinks and dentures become loose, requiring relining or replacement. Implants prevent this bone shrinkage.

**Do implant-supported dentures feel different from regular dentures?**
Yes, implant-supported dentures are much more stable. They do not slip, click, or cause sore spots. Patients report a significant improvement in chewing comfort and confidence compared to regular dentures.

**Can I sleep with implant-supported dentures in?**
You should remove implant-supported dentures at night for cleaning and to let your gums rest. Fixed full-arch implants (All-on-4) can be left in permanently since they are not removable.

## What are dental sealants?

Dental sealants are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth. They fill the deep grooves and create a smooth surface that is easier to clean and less likely to trap food.

## How do sealants work?

Sealants act as a barrier between the tooth and bacteria. They:

- Fill deep pits and grooves
- Prevent food from getting stuck
- Make chewing surfaces easier to clean
- Reduce the risk of cavities by up to 80%

## The sealant procedure

### Step 1: Cleaning

The tooth is cleaned and dried thoroughly.

### Step 2: Conditioning

A special gel is applied to roughen the tooth surface slightly. This helps the sealant bond.

### Step 3: Rinsing and drying

The gel is rinsed off and the tooth is dried again.

### Step 4: Sealant application

The liquid sealant is painted onto the chewing surface.

### Step 5: Curing

A special light hardens the sealant in seconds.

The whole procedure takes only a few minutes per tooth and is completely painless.

## How long do sealants last?

Sealants can last 5 to 10 years with proper care. Dr. Dash checks sealants during regular checkups and reapplies them if they wear down.

## Are sealants safe?

Yes, dental sealants are safe. They are made from dental resin and contain very low levels of BPA, which is not considered harmful. The benefits of preventing cavities far outweigh any minimal risk.

The American Dental Association and the Indian Dental Association both endorse dental sealants as a safe and effective preventive treatment for children.

## Sealants and fluoride: how they work together

Sealants and fluoride protect teeth in different ways:

| Protection | Sealants | Fluoride |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What it protects | Chewing surfaces of back teeth | All tooth surfaces |
| How it works | Physical barrier | Strengthens enamel |
| Best for | Grooves and pits | Smooth surfaces |
| Applied by | Dentist | Toothpaste, water, dentist |

Both are important for comprehensive cavity prevention. Dr. Dash recommends both sealants and fluoride treatment for children at high risk of cavities.

## When sealants may need reapplication

Sealants can wear down over time, especially if your child:

- Grinds their teeth
- Chews hard foods frequently
- Eats sticky candies regularly
- Has habits like chewing on pens

Dr. Dash checks sealants at every 6-month checkup. If a sealant is chipped or worn, she can reapply it quickly and painlessly.

Sealants are recommended for:

- Children and teenagers when permanent molars erupt

- Adults who are at high risk of cavities and have not had sealants

## Caring for teeth with sealants

- Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
- Floss daily
- Avoid chewing hard objects like ice or pens
- Avoid sticky candies that can pull sealants off
- Visit the dentist every 6 months

## Frequently asked questions

**At what age should children get sealants?**
Children should get sealants when their permanent molars come in, usually around ages 6 and 12.

**Are sealants painful?**
No, sealant application is completely painless and does not require drilling or anaesthesia.

**How much do sealants cost?**
Sealants cost ₹500 to ₹1,500 per tooth at YourDentist.

**Do sealants really prevent cavities?**
Yes, sealants can reduce the risk of cavities on molars by up to 80%.

**Can adults get sealants?**
Yes, adults with deep grooves and no decay can also benefit from sealants.

**Do sealants contain BPA?**
Sealants contain trace amounts of BPA, but the level is extremely low and not considered harmful. The protective benefits far outweigh any minimal risk.

**Can sealants come off?**
Yes, sealants can wear down or come off over time. Dr. Dash checks them at every checkup and can reapply them if needed.

**Are sealants visible on teeth?**
Sealants are clear or tooth-coloured and are not noticeable when your child smiles or talks.

**Can my child eat normally after getting sealants?**
Yes, your child can eat normally immediately after the procedure. There are no restrictions.

**How are sealants different from fluoride treatment?**
Sealants protect the chewing surfaces by creating a physical barrier over grooves and pits. Fluoride strengthens all tooth surfaces by making enamel more resistant to acid. Both work together for complete protection. Dr. Dash recommends both for children at high cavity risk.

**Can sealants be applied over early cavities?**
In some cases, sealants can be applied over very early, non-cavitated lesions to stop progression. Dr. Dash will assess whether the decay has gone too deep for sealant application. If it has, a filling is needed instead.

**Are sealants covered by insurance?**
Some dental insurance plans cover sealants for children under a certain age. We provide detailed invoices with procedure codes for insurance submission. Check with your provider for coverage details.

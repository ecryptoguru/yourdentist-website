## Do braces hurt when they are first put on?

The actual process of placing braces is not painful. The teeth are cleaned, the brackets are bonded, and the wires are attached. You may feel pressure as the wire is tightened, but it should not hurt.

The soreness usually starts a few hours after the braces are placed. It peaks in the first 2 to 3 days and then decreases.

## What does braces pain feel like?

Braces discomfort is usually:

- A dull ache in the teeth and jaw
- Soreness when chewing
- Tenderness in the teeth
- Irritation on the inside of the cheeks and lips from the brackets

This is different from the sharp pain of a toothache. It is a general soreness that comes from the pressure needed to move teeth.

## Braces pain timeline

| Time | What to expect |
| --- | --- |
| First few hours | Mild pressure, no major pain |
| Day 1 to 3 | Peak soreness, teeth feel tender |
| Day 4 to 7 | Soreness decreases |
| After adjustments | Mild soreness for 1 to 3 days |
| After first month | Most patients are used to braces |

## First week with braces

The first week is the hardest because your mouth is adjusting to the brackets and wires. During this time:

- Eat soft foods
- Use orthodontic wax on irritating brackets
- Rinse with warm salt water
- Take pain medication if needed
- Avoid hard and sticky foods

Most patients feel much more comfortable by the end of the first week.

## Managing braces pain at home

There are several ways to reduce discomfort from braces:

### Eat soft foods

Soft foods reduce pressure on sore teeth. Good options include:

- Yogurt
- Mashed potatoes
- Soup
- Khichdi
- Idli
- Bananas
- Scrambled eggs
- Smoothies

### Use orthodontic wax

Orthodontic wax covers brackets that rub against the cheeks and lips. Apply a small amount to the irritating bracket. This prevents sores and irritation.

### Rinse with warm salt water

A warm salt water rinse soothes irritated gums and mouth sores. Mix half a teaspoon of salt in warm water and rinse for 30 seconds.

### Take pain relievers

Over-the-counter pain medication can help with soreness, especially after adjustments. Take it as directed.

### Use a cold compress

A cold compress on the cheeks can reduce soreness and inflammation after braces placement or adjustment.

### Keep your mouth clean

Food stuck around brackets can irritate the gums. Brush carefully after every meal and use an interdental brush to clean around brackets.

## Do braces adjustments hurt?

After each adjustment, you may feel mild soreness for 1 to 3 days. This happens because the wire is tightened or changed, which increases pressure on the teeth. The soreness is usually less intense than the first week.

## Do clear aligners hurt?

Clear aligners can also cause discomfort, especially when switching to a new set. The pressure is usually focused on specific teeth that are moving. Aligner discomfort is usually less than braces because there are no brackets or wires rubbing against the cheeks.

## When to call your orthodontist

Most braces discomfort is normal. Contact your dentist if you have:

- Severe pain that does not improve with medication
- A wire poking into your cheek that you cannot fix with wax
- A broken bracket
- A loose wire
- Swollen gums with pus
- Mouth sores that do not heal

## Pain management comparison for braces

| Method | How it helps | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soft foods | Reduces chewing pressure | First week and after adjustments |
| Orthodontic wax | Prevents cheek and lip irritation | Whenever brackets rub |
| Salt water rinse | Soothes gums and mouth sores | 2 to 3 times daily |
| Over-the-counter painkillers | Reduces soreness and inflammation | After adjustments, as directed |
| Cold compress | Reduces swelling and numbness | First 24 hours after placement |
| Warm compress | Relieves jaw soreness | After first few days |
| Soft diet | Less pressure on teeth | First week, after tightening |

Using a combination of these methods provides the best relief during braces treatment.

## Braces discomfort by treatment type

| Treatment type | Pain level | Duration of soreness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Metal braces | Moderate | 3 to 5 days initially |
| Ceramic braces | Moderate | 3 to 5 days initially |
| Self-ligating braces | Mild to moderate | 2 to 4 days initially |
| Clear aligners | Mild | 1 to 3 days per new tray |

Self-ligating braces and clear aligners generally cause less discomfort than traditional metal or ceramic braces because they use lighter forces.

## Can braces pain be avoided?

Some soreness is unavoidable because teeth need pressure to move. However, you can reduce discomfort by following your dentist's instructions, keeping your mouth clean, and using wax and salt water rinses.

## Braces pain vs toothache pain

Braces soreness is a dull, pressure-like ache that affects multiple teeth. Toothache pain is usually sharp, localised, and constant. If you have toothache pain, it may be from a cavity or infection, not from the braces.

## Will braces hurt for the entire treatment?

No. Most patients adjust within a month. After that, braces feel like a normal part of life. There may be brief soreness after adjustments, but it is manageable.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do braces hurt when you first get them?**
There may be soreness for a few days, but the actual placement is not painful.

**How long do braces hurt after tightening?**
Soreness after tightening usually lasts 1 to 3 days.

**How can I stop braces from hurting?**
Eat soft foods, use orthodontic wax, rinse with salt water, and take pain medication if needed.

**Are clear aligners less painful than braces?**
Aligners usually cause less cheek irritation but can still cause pressure soreness when switching trays.

**Do braces hurt for the whole treatment?**
No. Most patients adjust within the first month.

**What should I eat when my braces hurt?**
Soft foods like yogurt, soup, khichdi, and mashed potatoes are good choices.

**Can I take painkillers before getting braces?**
Taking an over-the-counter pain reliever about an hour before your appointment can help reduce initial soreness. Ask Dr. Dash for specific recommendations.

**Does braces pain mean something is wrong?**
No. Mild soreness is normal and means the braces are working. However, sharp or severe pain is not normal and should be reported to your dentist.

**How long does it take to get used to braces?**
Most patients adjust to braces within 1 to 2 weeks. After that, the brackets feel like a normal part of your mouth, and you only experience mild soreness after adjustments.

**Can children handle braces pain?**
Yes, children are often more adaptable than adults. Dr. Dash uses gentle techniques for children and provides clear instructions for managing discomfort at home.

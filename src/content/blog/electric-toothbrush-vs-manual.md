## Benefits of electric toothbrushes

### Better plaque removal

Studies show that electric toothbrushes remove more plaque than manual brushes, especially around the gumline and between teeth.

### Built-in timer

Most electric toothbrushes have a 2-minute timer to ensure you brush long enough.

### Less effort

The brush does the work. You just guide it along your teeth. This is helpful for people with limited dexterity.

### Pressure sensors

Some electric brushes alert you if you are brushing too hard. This helps prevent enamel wear and gum recession.

### Good for children

Electric brushes can make brushing more fun for children and help them brush for the full 2 minutes.

### Helpful for braces

Oscillating electric brushes can clean around brackets and wires more effectively than manual brushes.

## Benefits of manual toothbrushes

### Affordable

Manual toothbrushes are inexpensive and widely available.

### No charging needed

They do not need batteries or charging, making them ideal for travel.

### Good technique matters most

A manual brush with proper brushing technique can clean teeth just as well as a basic electric brush.

### Easy to replace

You replace the whole brush every 3 months.

## Who should use an electric toothbrush?

Electric toothbrushes are especially helpful for:

- People with braces
- Children who do not brush long enough
- Older adults with limited hand movement
- People with arthritis
- People with gum disease
- People who brush too hard
- People who want better plaque removal

## Who can use a manual toothbrush?

Manual toothbrushes are fine for:

- People with good brushing technique
- Those on a tight budget
- Frequent travellers
- People who prefer simple tools

## How to use an electric toothbrush

1. Apply a pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste.
2. Place the brush head at a 45-degree angle to the gumline.
3. Turn on the brush and let it do the work.
4. Gently move from tooth to tooth.
5. Brush for the full 2 minutes.
6. Do not press too hard.
7. Rinse the brush head after use.

## How often should you replace your toothbrush?

- Replace manual toothbrushes every 3 months
- Replace electric toothbrush heads every 3 months
- Replace sooner if bristles are frayed
- Replace after illness

## Is an electric toothbrush worth the cost?

For most people, yes. Electric toothbrushes provide better plaque removal and encourage longer brushing. However, a manual brush with proper technique is also effective if budget is a concern.

## Electric toothbrush features to look for

- Pressure sensor to prevent brushing too hard
- 2-minute timer with 30-second interval alerts
- Multiple brushing modes (clean, sensitive, whitening)
- Long battery life for travel
- Replaceable brush heads
- Waterproof design
- Soft bristles to protect gums and enamel

## Brushing tips regardless of brush type

- Brush twice daily for 2 minutes
- Use a soft-bristle brush
- Hold at a 45-degree angle to gums
- Use gentle circular motions
- Brush all surfaces: outer, inner, and chewing
- Do not forget the back teeth
- Replace your brush regularly

## Frequently asked questions

**Are electric toothbrushes better than manual?**
Electric toothbrushes remove more plaque and are easier to use correctly, but manual brushes can be effective with proper technique.

**How much do electric toothbrushes cost in India?**
Electric toothbrushes cost ₹1,500 to ₹10,000 or more depending on features.

**Can children use electric toothbrushes?**
Yes, children's electric toothbrushes are available and can make brushing more fun.

**How often should I change my electric toothbrush head?**
Every 3 months, or sooner if bristles become frayed.

**Do electric toothbrushes damage teeth?**
No, if used correctly. Some models have pressure sensors to prevent brushing too hard.

**Can I use an electric toothbrush with sensitive teeth?**
Yes, most electric toothbrushes have a sensitive mode. Use soft bristle heads and avoid pressing hard.

**How long does an electric toothbrush battery last?**
Most electric toothbrushes last 1 to 3 weeks on a single charge, depending on usage and model.

**Are expensive electric toothbrushes worth it?**
Mid-range models with pressure sensors and timers offer the best value. Premium models add features like Bluetooth tracking and multiple modes, but the cleaning benefit is marginal.

**Can I share an electric toothbrush handle?**
Yes, but each person must use their own brush head. Sharing brush heads is not hygienic and can spread bacteria.

**Can I travel with an electric toothbrush?**
Yes, most electric toothbrushes come with travel cases. Charge it fully before travel. Sonic brushes typically last 2 to 3 weeks on a single charge, making them ideal for travel.

**Should I use an electric toothbrush if I have implants?**
Yes, electric toothbrushes are safe for implants. Use a soft brush head and avoid pressing hard. Dr. Dash can recommend specific brush heads designed for implant care.

**Can electric toothbrushes whiten teeth?**
Electric toothbrushes with whitening modes can remove surface stains more effectively than manual brushes. However, they cannot change the natural colour of your teeth. For significant whitening, professional treatment at YourDentist is needed.

## Why some foods are bad for braces

Braces are strong, but they are not indestructible. The brackets are bonded to your teeth with adhesive. Hard or sticky foods can:

- Pop brackets off the tooth
- Bend or break wires
- Loosen bands
- Get stuck between brackets and wires
- Increase the risk of cavities
- Cause discomfort

Broken brackets and wires mean extra visits and can extend treatment time.

## Foods to avoid with braces

### Hard foods

- Nuts and seeds
- Ice cubes
- Hard candy
- Popcorn kernels
- Raw carrots and apples (unless cut into small pieces)
- Corn chips and hard tacos
- Pizza crust
- Crusty bread
- Bones and hard meat

### Sticky foods

- Caramel and toffee
- Chewing gum
- Gummy bears and jelly candies
- Taffy
- Fruit roll-ups
- Chocolate with caramel fillings
- Sticky rice dishes that are hard to chew

### Chewy foods

- Bagels and hard rolls
- Beef jerky
- Tough meats
- Licorice
- Dried fruits like raisins and dates
- Chewy candy

### Sugary foods and drinks

- Sodas and energy drinks
- Sugary juices
- Candy
- Sweets and mithai
- Flavoured milk

These increase the risk of cavities around brackets.

### Foods that stain

- Coffee
- Tea
- Red wine
- Turmeric-heavy curries
- Coloured sauces
- Beetroot

These can stain clear brackets and elastic ties.

## Foods you can eat with braces

### Soft foods

- Yogurt
- Mashed potatoes
- Rice and dal
- Khichdi
- Idli and dosa
- Soft chapati
- Pasta
- Soup
- Scrambled eggs
- Bananas
- Applesauce
- Cottage cheese and paneer

### Proteins

- Fish
- Soft chicken
- Eggs
- Paneer
- Tofu
- Lentils and beans

### Vegetables and fruits

- Cooked vegetables
- Soft fruits like bananas and melons
- Steamed carrots and broccoli
- Avocado
- Small pieces of soft apple

### Grains

- Soft bread
- Rice
- Pasta
- Oatmeal
- Porridge

### Dairy

- Milk
- Cheese
- Yogurt
- Paneer

## How to eat with braces

Even safe foods should be eaten carefully:

- Cut hard foods into small pieces.
- Chew with your back teeth.
- Avoid biting directly into hard fruits or vegetables.
- Eat slowly to avoid accidental damage.
- Rinse your mouth after eating.
- Brush after meals when possible.

## What to do if a bracket breaks from food

If a bracket or wire breaks:

1. Do not panic. Broken brackets are common.
2. Cover any sharp area with orthodontic wax.
3. Call YourDentist to book a repair appointment.
4. Avoid hard foods until the repair is done.

## Can you eat with clear aligners?

No. Clear aligners must be removed before eating or drinking anything except water. Eating with aligners can damage them and stain the plastic. Brush your teeth before putting aligners back in.

## Foods that help braces treatment

Some foods can support oral health during orthodontic treatment:

- Calcium-rich foods like milk and cheese strengthen enamel.
- Soft fruits and vegetables provide vitamins.
- Water rinses away food particles.
- Sugar-free gum with xylitol can help clean teeth (only if your dentist allows).

## Drinks to avoid with braces

- Sugary sodas
- Energy drinks
- Citrus juices in excess
- Dark-coloured drinks that stain
- Very hot drinks that can soften adhesive

Water is the best drink during braces treatment.

## Why following food rules matters

Following the food guidelines helps:

- Prevent broken brackets and wires
- Reduce emergency visits
- Keep treatment on schedule
- Protect teeth from decay and staining
- Maintain gum health

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I eat pizza with braces?**
Soft pizza without a hard crust is fine. Avoid crunchy crusts.

**Can I eat chocolate with braces?**
Plain chocolate that melts easily is fine. Avoid chocolate with caramel, nuts, or hard fillings.

**Can I eat rice with braces?**
Yes, soft rice is one of the best foods with braces.

**Can I drink coffee with braces?**
Yes, but coffee can stain clear brackets and teeth. Rinse or brush after drinking.

**Can I chew gum with braces?**
Regular chewing gum is not recommended. Sugar-free gum may be allowed if your dentist approves.

**What is the worst food for braces?**
Hard candy, caramel, nuts, and popcorn are among the worst foods for braces.

**Can I eat ice cream with braces?**
Yes, ice cream is safe for braces. Avoid varieties with nuts, caramel chunks, or hard mix-ins.

**How soon after getting braces can I eat normally?**
For the first 3 to 5 days after getting braces, stick to soft foods. After that, you can eat most foods with care, avoiding hard and sticky items throughout treatment.

**What should I eat if my braces hurt after tightening?**
Eat soft foods like yogurt, soup, mashed potatoes, smoothies, and scrambled eggs for 1 to 2 days after adjustments. Soreness usually subsides within a few days.

**Can I eat non-vegetarian food with braces?**
Yes, but avoid bones and tough meats. Fish, soft chicken, and minced meat are good options. Cut everything into small pieces.

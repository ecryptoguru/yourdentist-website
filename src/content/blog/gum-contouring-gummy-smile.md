## What is a gummy smile?

A gummy smile is defined as showing more than 3 to 4 mm of gum tissue when smiling fully. It can be caused by:

- Excess gum tissue covering the teeth
- Short upper lip that lifts too high
- Overactive upper lip muscles
- Teeth that are short due to wear
- Upper jaw that is positioned too far forward

Gum contouring addresses the excess gum tissue cause.

## What is gum contouring?

Gum contouring, also called gum reshaping or gingival contouring, is a procedure that removes excess gum tissue to expose more of the tooth. This creates a more balanced and attractive gum line.

At YourDentist, gum contouring is done with a dental laser, which means:

- No cutting with a scalpel
- Minimal bleeding
- Faster healing
- More precise results
- Less discomfort

## The laser gum contouring procedure

### Step 1: Consultation and assessment

Dr. Dash examines your smile, measures the gum line, and determines how much tissue needs to be reshaped. Photographs are taken for before-and-after comparison.

### Step 2: Anaesthesia

Local anaesthesia is applied to numb the gum area. This ensures the procedure is painless.

### Step 3: Laser reshaping

Dr. Dash uses a dental laser to precisely remove excess gum tissue. The laser seals blood vessels as it cuts, so there is minimal bleeding. The laser also sterilises the area, reducing infection risk.

### Step 4: Shaping and polishing

The gum line is sculpted to create a natural, even curve. The teeth appear longer and more proportionate.

### Step 5: Final check

Dr. Dash checks the symmetry and balance of the new gum line. Adjustments are made if needed.

The entire procedure takes 30 to 60 minutes.

## What is a dental implant made of?

A dental implant has three main parts:

| Part | Material | Lifespan |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Implant fixture | Titanium | 15 to 25+ years |
| Abutment | Titanium or zirconia | 15 to 25+ years |
| Crown | Ceramic, zirconia, or metal-ceramic | 10 to 15 years |

The titanium implant fuses with the jawbone through a process called osseointegration. This makes it extremely stable.

## Average lifespan of dental implants

Research and clinical experience show that dental implants have a success rate of 95% to 98% over 10 years. Many patients keep their implants for 20 to 30 years or more. The implant itself rarely fails. Most failures happen in the first year, usually due to poor healing or infection.

## Factors that affect how long implants last

### Oral hygiene

Patients who brush twice daily, floss, and visit the dentist regularly have the best outcomes. Poor hygiene leads to peri-implantitis, an infection around the implant that can cause failure.

### Smoking

Smoking reduces blood flow to the gums and bone, slowing healing and increasing the risk of implant failure. Smokers have a higher implant failure rate than non-smokers.

### Bone quality

Implants placed in strong, healthy bone last longer. Patients with thin or soft bone may need grafting before implant placement.

### Medical conditions

Uncontrolled diabetes, osteoporosis, and autoimmune diseases can affect healing and implant success. These conditions do not rule out implants, but they need careful management.

### Implant placement and planning

Proper planning using CBCT scans, digital impressions, and careful surgical technique improves implant longevity. Poorly placed implants are more likely to fail.

### Bite forces

Grinding or clenching teeth puts excess force on implants and crowns. A nightguard can protect implants in patients with bruxism.

### Crown material and fit

A well-fitting crown distributes forces evenly. Poorly fitting crowns can overload the implant and cause problems over time.

## How to make dental implants last longer

1. **Brush twice daily** with a soft-bristle brush.
2. **Floss or use interdental brushes** around the implant.
3. **Use a water flosser** to clean hard-to-reach areas.
4. **Visit the dentist every six months** for checkups and cleanings.
5. **Avoid smoking** before and after implant treatment.
6. **Do not chew hard objects** like ice, pens, or bones.
7. **Wear a nightguard** if you grind your teeth.
8. **Treat gum disease** promptly if it develops.

## What is peri-implantitis?

Peri-implantitis is an infection and inflammation around an implant. It is similar to gum disease. If untreated, it can destroy the bone supporting the implant. Signs include:

- Red or swollen gums around the implant
- Bleeding when brushing
- Bad taste or smell
- Pain or tenderness
- Visible implant threads

Regular checkups catch peri-implantitis early.

## Do implants need to be replaced?

The implant itself usually does not need replacement. The crown, abutment, or screw may need maintenance over time. Crowns typically last 10 to 15 years, depending on wear and care.

## Signs your implant needs attention

- Pain or swelling around the implant
- Loose crown or abutment
- Gum recession exposing the implant
- Difficulty chewing
- Unusual sounds from the implant

If you notice any of these, see your dentist immediately.

## Implant maintenance schedule

| Time after placement | What to do | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First 2 weeks | Soft diet, gentle cleaning, no smoking | Allow initial healing |
| 1 to 3 months | Continue soft foods, attend follow-up | Monitor osseointegration |
| 3 to 6 months | Crown placement, normal brushing | Restore full function |
| Every 6 months | Professional cleaning and checkup | Prevent peri-implantitis |
| Annually | X-ray to check bone levels | Detect early bone loss |
| Every 10 to 15 years | Crown may need replacement | Normal wear and tear |

Following this maintenance schedule gives your implant the best chance of lasting a lifetime.

## Factors that shorten implant lifespan

| Factor | Impact | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Smoking | 2x failure rate | Quit before and after surgery |
| Poor oral hygiene | Plaque and infection | Brush, floss, water flosser |
| Teeth grinding | Overload and loosening | Wear nightguard |
| Uncontrolled diabetes | Slow healing, infection | Control blood sugar |
| Skipping checkups | Undetected problems | Visit every 6 months |
| Hard chewing | Crown fracture | Avoid ice, bones, hard objects |

Most factors that shorten implant lifespan are preventable with good habits and regular dental visits.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long do dental implants last?**
Dental implants can last 15 to 25 years or more with proper care.

**Do dental implant crowns need to be replaced?**
Yes, crowns typically last 10 to 15 years and may need replacement due to wear.

**Can implants last a lifetime?**
Many implants last a lifetime, especially with good oral hygiene and regular checkups.

**What reduces implant lifespan?**
Smoking, poor oral hygiene, gum disease, and teeth grinding can reduce implant lifespan.

**How do I care for my dental implant?**
Brush twice daily, floss around the implant, and visit your dentist regularly.

**Is dental implant failure common?**
Implant failure is rare. The success rate is 95% to 98% over 10 years.

**Can I chew normally with a dental implant?**
Yes, after the implant has fully healed and the permanent crown is placed, you can chew normally. Implants restore nearly 100% of natural chewing force.

**How often should I get my implant checked?**
We recommend a professional checkup and cleaning every 6 months. Dr. Dash may recommend annual X-rays to monitor bone levels around the implant.

**What happens when the crown wears out?**
The crown can be replaced without removing the implant. The old crown is removed, and a new one is fitted to the existing abutment or a new abutment if needed.

**Do implants require special cleaning products?**
No special products are needed. A soft toothbrush, regular fluoride toothpaste, dental floss, and optionally a water flosser are sufficient. Interdental brushes can help clean around the implant.

**Can a dental implant fail after many years?**
Yes, late failure can occur due to peri-implantitis, overload, or medical conditions. However, with proper care and regular checkups, the risk is very low.

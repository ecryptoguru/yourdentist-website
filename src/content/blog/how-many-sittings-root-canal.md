One of the most common questions patients ask about root canal treatment is, "How many visits will I need?" The answer depends on the tooth, the infection, and the treatment approach. At YourDentist Laser Dental Clinic in Bhubaneswar, most root canals are completed in a single sitting. Some cases need two or more visits. Advances in rotary endodontics and laser disinfection have made single-sitting RCT the standard for most cases.

In Bhubaneswar, many patients put off root canal treatment because they fear it will require multiple painful visits over weeks. Understanding that most cases can be completed in one sitting helps patients seek timely treatment instead of delaying until the infection worsens.

This guide explains single-sitting and multi-sitting root canals, when each is used, and what to expect.

## Single-sitting root canal treatment

Single-sitting root canal treatment means the entire procedure is completed in one appointment. The dentist removes the infected pulp, cleans and disinfects the canals, fills them, and seals the tooth in one visit. A temporary or permanent filling is placed, and the patient returns later for the crown.

At YourDentist, single-sitting root canal is the standard for most cases because of our rotary endodontics and laser-assisted disinfection.

## Multiple-sitting root canal treatment

Multi-sitting root canal treatment divides the procedure across two or more visits. The first visit focuses on cleaning and medication. The second visit completes the filling and sealing. This approach is used when the infection needs more time to settle.

## Single-sitting vs multi-sitting comparison

| Factor | Single-sitting RCT | Multi-sitting RCT |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Number of visits | One | Two or more |
| Total chair time | 60 to 90 minutes | 30 to 60 minutes per visit |
| Anesthesia injections | Usually one | One per visit |
| Infection control | Completed same day | Medication placed between visits |
| Convenience | More convenient | More visits required |
| Cost | Often lower overall | May cost slightly more due to extra visits |
| Success rate | High for suitable cases | High for complex infections |

## When is single-sitting root canal possible?

Single-sitting RCT is possible when:

- The infection is localised and not severe
- There is no significant swelling or abscess
- The canals can be cleaned and dried completely
- The patient can tolerate the full procedure
- The tooth has normal canal anatomy

Most front teeth and premolars fall into this category. Many molars can also be treated in one sitting.

## When is multi-sitting root canal needed?

Multi-sitting RCT may be needed when:

- There is severe infection with swelling
- Pus is draining from the tooth
- The infection has spread to surrounding bone
- The canals are very narrow or blocked
- The patient has severe pain that makes a long appointment difficult
- The tooth needs medication between visits to control infection

In these cases, the dentist places medication inside the tooth and seals it with a temporary filling. The patient returns after a few days to complete the root canal.

## How many sittings are needed for different teeth?

| Tooth type | Typical sittings |
| --- | --- |
| Front tooth | 1 |
| Premolar | 1 to 2 |
| Molar | 1 to 2 |
| Retreatment case | 2 to 3 |
| Severe infection | 2 to 3 |

These are general guidelines. The final decision is made after examination and X-rays.

## Benefits of single-sitting root canal

- Saves time for the patient
- Reduces the number of injections
- Lowers risk of contamination between visits
- Faster relief from pain
- Fewer travel trips for patients from Cuttack, KIIT, or nearby areas
- More convenient for working patients

## Benefits of multi-sitting root canal

- Allows medication to control infection
- Reduces pressure inside severely infected teeth
- Better for complex cases with abscess
- Gives the dentist time to assess healing
- Can be less tiring for patients who cannot sit for long

## What happens between multi-sitting visits

| What happens | Why it matters | Patient instructions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Medication placed in canals | Kills remaining bacteria | Do not remove temporary filling |
| Temporary filling seals tooth | Prevents contamination | Avoid hard/sticky foods |
| Antibiotics may be prescribed | Controls spreading infection | Take full course as directed |
| Pain medication as needed | Manages discomfort | Take as prescribed |
| 3 to 7 day gap between visits | Allows medication to work | Keep area clean, avoid chewing on that side |

Following these instructions between visits ensures the infection is fully controlled before the canal is sealed, leading to a successful outcome.

## What to expect in a single-sitting root canal

The procedure takes 45 to 90 minutes. You receive local anesthesia, the tooth is isolated, the canals are cleaned and filled, and the tooth is sealed. You may receive a temporary filling and return later for a permanent crown.

## What to expect in a multi-sitting root canal

### First visit

- Numbing and isolation
- Opening the tooth
- Removing infected pulp
- Cleaning the canals
- Placing medication
- Temporary filling

### Second visit

- Removing temporary filling
- Checking for healing
- Cleaning again if needed
- Filling and sealing canals
- Temporary or permanent filling

In some cases, a third visit is needed for complex infections.

## How to prepare for multiple visits

- Schedule visits close together to avoid infection returning.
- Take prescribed medication on time.
- Avoid chewing on the treated side between visits.
- Keep the area clean.
- Call the clinic if temporary filling falls out or pain worsens.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a root canal be done in one sitting?**
Yes, most root canals at YourDentist are completed in a single sitting.

**How many sittings are needed for molar root canal?**
Most molars can be treated in one sitting. Complex cases may need two visits.

**Why do some root canals need two sittings?**
Severe infection, swelling, or complex canal anatomy may require medication between visits.

**Is single-sitting root canal better?**
It is more convenient and reduces infection risk, but the dentist will choose the best approach for your case.

**How many days gap between two sittings?**
The gap is usually 3 to 7 days, depending on the infection.

**Does multi-sitting root canal cost more?**
It may cost slightly more due to additional visits and medication.

**Can I choose single-sitting even if my case is complex?**
Dr. Dash will assess your case and advise honestly. If single-sitting is not safe for your infection level, she will recommend multiple visits. Attempting single-sitting on a severe infection can lead to treatment failure.

**What if I cannot come back for a second sitting?**
If you are travelling from outside Bhubaneswar, tell Dr. Dash during your consultation. She can plan treatment to minimise visits or schedule visits close together. However, do not skip a required second visit, as this risks infection returning.

**Is the temporary filling between visits safe?**
Yes, the temporary filling is designed to seal the tooth between visits. However, avoid hard, sticky, or chewy foods on that side. If the temporary filling falls out, call YourDentist immediately for a replacement.

**How long does each sitting take?**
A single-sitting root canal takes 45 to 90 minutes. In multi-sitting treatment, the first visit takes 30 to 45 minutes and the second visit takes 30 to 45 minutes. The total time is similar, just spread across visits.

## Why cleaning with braces is harder

Brackets and wires create small spaces where plaque builds up. Food particles get stuck around brackets and under wires. If not removed, plaque hardens into tartar and causes:

- Cavities around brackets
- White spots on teeth
- Gum inflammation
- Bad breath
- Staining
- Delayed treatment due to unhealthy gums

Good cleaning habits prevent these problems.

## What you need to clean teeth with braces

- Soft-bristle toothbrush or electric toothbrush
- Fluoride toothpaste
- Interdental brush
- Floss threaders or orthodontic floss
- Mouthwash (optional)
- Orthodontic wax
- Mirror (helpful for checking)

## Step-by-step brushing with braces

### Step 1: Rinse with water

Rinse your mouth with water to loosen food particles before brushing.

### Step 2: Brush at a 45-degree angle

Hold the toothbrush at a 45-degree angle to the gumline. Brush in small circular motions. Clean the area above and below the brackets.

### Step 3: Brush each bracket

Brush directly on each bracket. Angle the brush from the top and then from the bottom to clean around the wires. Spend extra time on areas where food collects.

### Step 4: Brush the inner and chewing surfaces

Do not forget the inner surfaces of your teeth and the chewing surfaces. These areas also collect plaque.

### Step 5: Brush for two minutes

Brush for at least two minutes, twice a day. An electric toothbrush with a timer can help.

### Step 6: Rinse thoroughly

Rinse with water or fluoride mouthwash to remove loosened plaque.

## How to floss with braces

Flossing with braces is challenging but important. Here are the methods:

### Floss threader method

1. Thread a piece of floss through the floss threader.
2. Slide the threader under the wire.
3. Pull the floss through and floss between the teeth.
4. Repeat for each tooth.

### Orthodontic floss

Orthodontic floss has a stiff end that can be pushed under the wire without a threader.

### Water flosser

A water flosser uses a stream of water to clean between teeth and around braces. It is helpful for people who find traditional flossing difficult.

## Using an interdental brush

An interdental brush is a small brush that fits between brackets and under wires. It removes plaque and food that regular brushing can miss. Use it once a day, especially after meals.

## How often should you clean your teeth with braces?

- **Brush:** At least twice a day, ideally after every meal.
- **Floss:** Once a day.
- **Interdental brush:** Once a day or after meals.
- **Mouthwash:** Once a day if recommended.

## Foods to avoid with braces

Some foods can damage braces or get stuck. Avoid:

- Hard foods like nuts and ice
- Sticky foods like caramel and chewing gum
- Crunchy foods like popcorn and chips
- Chewy foods like hard candy
- Whole fruits like apples (cut into small pieces instead)
- Corn on the cob (cut off the cob)

## Signs of poor oral hygiene with braces

Watch for these warning signs:

- Red or swollen gums
- Bleeding when brushing
- White spots near brackets
- Bad breath
- Yellow or brown staining
- Loose brackets

If you notice these, improve your cleaning routine and see your dentist.

## Common mistakes when cleaning braces

- Brushing too hard, which can damage gums
- Not brushing long enough
- Skipping flossing
- Using a hard-bristle brush
- Not cleaning around the gumline
- Ignoring the inner surfaces of teeth

## Cleaning schedule for braces patients

| Time | Task |
| --- | --- |
| Morning | Brush and floss |
| After breakfast | Rinse or brush |
| After lunch | Rinse and use interdental brush |

## Frequently asked questions

**How do I brush my teeth with braces?**
Brush at a 45-degree angle to the gumline and around each bracket for two minutes, twice a day.

**How often should I brush with braces?**
At least twice a day, ideally after every meal.

**Can I use a normal toothbrush with braces?**
A soft-bristle toothbrush or electric toothbrush works best. Some patients prefer an orthodontic brush.

**How do I floss with braces?**
Use a floss threader, orthodontic floss, or water flosser to clean between teeth under the wires.

**What happens if I don't clean my teeth with braces?**
Poor hygiene can cause cavities, gum disease, white spots, and bad breath.

**Can I eat normally with braces?**
You can eat most foods, but avoid hard, sticky, and chewy foods that can damage braces.

**Can I use mouthwash with braces?**
Yes, a fluoride mouthwash is recommended for braces patients. It helps strengthen enamel and reach areas that brushing may miss. Ask Dr. Dash for a specific recommendation.

**How long does it take to clean teeth with braces?**
Plan for 10 to 15 minutes per session, including brushing, flossing, and using an interdental brush. This is longer than without braces but is necessary for good oral health.

**What is a water flosser and do I need one?**
A water flosser uses a stream of water to clean between teeth and around braces. It is highly effective and recommended for braces patients who find traditional flossing difficult.

**Will braces leave marks on my teeth?**
With proper cleaning, braces will not leave marks. However, poor oral hygiene can cause white spot lesions (permanent white marks) around brackets. This is preventable with good cleaning habits.

## 1. Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste

Brushing removes plaque and food particles. Fluoride strengthens enamel and helps repair early tooth decay. Brush for two minutes, twice a day, especially before bed.

## 2. Floss every day

Flossing removes plaque between teeth where brushing cannot reach. Cavities often start in these tight spaces. Floss at least once daily.

## 3. Limit sugar and sugary drinks

Bacteria feed on sugar and produce acids that cause cavities. Reduce:

- Chocolates and candies
- Soft drinks and energy drinks
- Sweetened tea and coffee
- Fruit juices
- Sticky snacks like toffees and dried fruit

If you consume sugar, rinse your mouth with water afterwards.

## 4. Avoid frequent snacking

Every time you eat, acids attack your teeth for 20 to 30 minutes. Frequent snacking keeps your teeth under constant acid attack. Eat meals at regular times and limit snacks.

## 5. Drink plenty of water

Water washes away food particles and bacteria. It also helps saliva neutralise acids. Drink water after meals and throughout the day.

## 6. Use fluoride mouthwash

Fluoride mouthwash helps strengthen enamel and reduce plaque. Use it once a day, preferably at a different time than brushing. Do not swallow mouthwash.

## 7. Eat tooth-friendly foods

Some foods help protect teeth:

- Cheese and milk (calcium for strong enamel)
- Leafy greens (vitamins and minerals)
- Nuts (protein and healthy fats)
- Apples and carrots (clean teeth as you chew)
- Sugar-free gum (stimulates saliva)

## 8. Get dental sealants

Dental sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth. They block food and bacteria from getting into deep grooves. Sealants are especially helpful for children.

## 9. Visit the dentist every 6 months

Regular checkups catch cavities early. Professional cleaning removes tartar that brushing cannot remove. Dr. Dash can also apply fluoride treatments if needed.

## 10. Consider fluoride treatment

Professional fluoride treatment strengthens enamel and makes teeth more resistant to acid. It is helpful for children, people with dry mouth, and those with a history of cavities.

## What causes cavities?

Cavities form when:

- Bacteria in plaque feed on sugar
- Acids are produced
- Acids dissolve enamel
- Decay reaches dentin and pulp

Poor oral hygiene, sugary diet, dry mouth, and lack of fluoride speed up this process.

## Signs of a cavity

- Toothache or sensitivity
- Pain when eating sweet, hot, or cold foods
- Visible holes or pits in teeth
- Brown, black, or white staining on teeth
- Bad breath

## Can cavities heal naturally?

Very early enamel decay can remineralise with fluoride and good hygiene. Once a cavity forms in dentin, it cannot heal naturally and needs a filling.

## How plaque becomes a cavity

Understanding the cavity process helps you see why prevention matters:

1. **Plaque forms:** Bacteria stick to teeth within hours of brushing
2. **Acid production:** Bacteria feed on sugar and produce acid
3. **Enamel demineralisation:** Acid dissolves minerals from enamel
4. **White spot lesions:** Early visible signs of decay appear
5. **Dentin involvement:** Decay reaches the softer inner layer
6. **Pulp infection:** Bacteria reach the nerve, causing pain

Fluoride and good hygiene can reverse steps 1 to 4. Once decay reaches dentin (step 5), professional treatment is needed.

## Cavity prevention for children

- Start cleaning teeth as soon as they erupt
- Avoid putting babies to bed with bottles
- Limit sugary snacks and drinks
- Supervise brushing until age 7
- Apply dental sealants on molars
- Take children for regular dental visits

## Frequently asked questions

**Can cavities be prevented completely?**
Yes, most cavities can be prevented with good oral hygiene, a low-sugar diet, fluoride, and regular dental visits.

**How often should I brush to prevent cavities?**
Brush twice daily for two minutes, and floss once a day.

**Do dental sealants prevent cavities?**
Yes, sealants reduce the risk of cavities on chewing surfaces by up to 80%.

**Is fluoride safe?**
Yes, fluoride is safe and effective when used as directed. It strengthens enamel and prevents cavities.

**What foods cause cavities?**
Sugary and starchy foods, especially sticky snacks and sugary drinks, are the main causes of cavities.

**Can dry mouth cause cavities?**
Yes, saliva helps neutralise acids and wash away food. Reduced saliva from medications or medical conditions increases cavity risk. Drink plenty of water and ask Dr. Dash about dry mouth solutions.

**Are cavities genetic?**
While genetics can affect tooth shape and enamel strength, most cavities are caused by diet and oral hygiene habits. Even with genetic risk, proper prevention can keep teeth healthy.

**How do I know if I have a cavity?**
Early cavities may have no symptoms. As decay progresses, you may feel sensitivity to sweet, hot, or cold foods, pain when chewing, or notice visible holes. Regular dental checkups catch cavities before symptoms appear.

**Can I prevent cavities without fluoride?**
Fluoride is the most effective cavity-prevention tool, but good hygiene, a low-sugar diet, sealants, and regular cleanings also help. However, combining all these with fluoride gives the best protection.

After a tooth extraction, one of the first questions patients ask is, "When can I get an implant?" The timing depends on the condition of the tooth, the bone, and the gums. There are three main options: immediate implant placement, early implant placement, and delayed implant placement. Research shows that up to 50% of jawbone width can be lost within the first year after extraction if no socket preservation is performed.

In Bhubaneswar, many patients delay implant planning after extraction, not realising that the jawbone shrinks rapidly once a tooth is removed. Early planning with Dr. Dash can preserve bone and reduce the need for extensive grafting later.

## Three timing options for implants after extraction

| Option | Timing | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Immediate implant | Same day as extraction | Front teeth, no infection, good bone |
| Early implant | 2 to 8 weeks after extraction | Mild infection, soft tissue healing needed |
| Delayed implant | 3 to 6 months after extraction | Severe infection, bone grafting needed |

## Immediate implant placement

The implant is placed into the socket immediately after tooth extraction. This is done in a single surgery.

### Advantages

- Fewer surgeries and visits
- No gap in your smile
- Faster overall treatment
- Bone is stimulated immediately, reducing bone loss

### Disadvantages

- Higher risk of failure if there is infection
- Not suitable for all cases
- Requires very precise technique

### When it is suitable

- No active infection in the tooth or surrounding bone
- Enough bone around the socket for implant stability
- Healthy gums
- Front teeth where aesthetics are important
- Non-smoker

## Early implant placement

The tooth is extracted and the site is allowed to heal for 2 to 8 weeks. This allows soft tissue (gum) to heal before the implant is placed.

### Advantages of early placement

- Gum is healed, making implant placement easier
- Reduced risk of infection compared to immediate placement
- Faster than delayed placement

### Disadvantages of early placement

- Two separate surgeries
- Some bone loss may occur during the waiting period
- Temporary tooth needed during healing

### When early placement is suitable

- Mild infection at the tooth root
- Gum needs to heal before implant
- Moderate bone loss that does not need grafting

## Delayed implant placement

The tooth is extracted and the site heals for 3 to 6 months before the implant is placed. This is the traditional approach.

### Advantages of delayed placement

- Complete healing of bone and gum
- Lowest risk of complications
- Suitable for almost all cases
- Allows time for bone grafting to integrate

### Disadvantages of delayed placement

- Longer total treatment time
- More bone loss during waiting period
- Temporary denture needed for longer
- Two or more surgeries

### When delayed placement is suitable

- Severe infection or abscess at the tooth root
- Significant bone loss requiring grafting
- Gum disease around the tooth
- Heavy smokers
- Medical conditions that slow healing

## Socket preservation after extraction

When a tooth is extracted and implant placement is delayed, the jawbone in that area shrinks. Socket preservation is a technique where bone graft material is placed into the socket immediately after extraction. This preserves the bone volume for future implant placement.

### Benefits of socket preservation

- Maintains bone height and width
- Reduces the need for extensive grafting later
- Improves implant success rate
- Better aesthetic outcome

| Time | Care instructions | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First 24 hours | Bite on gauze, avoid rinsing, ice pack | Controls bleeding and swelling |
| Days 2 to 7 | Soft diet, gentle rinsing, avoid smoking | Promotes gum healing |
| Weeks 2 to 4 | Normal diet on other side, maintain hygiene | Soft tissue healing completes |

Proper care during the healing period ensures the best conditions for successful implant placement.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I get an implant the same day as extraction?**
Yes, in suitable cases with no infection and good bone. Dr. Dash will assess if you qualify.

**How long should I wait after extraction before getting an implant?**
It depends on the case. Immediate placement is same day, early placement is 2 to 8 weeks, and delayed placement is 3 to 6 months.

**What is socket preservation?**
Socket preservation is placing bone graft material into the extraction socket to maintain bone volume for future implant placement.

**Will I have a gap in my smile while waiting for an implant?**
We provide temporary tooth options like flippers or retainers to fill the gap during healing.

**How much does an implant cost after extraction in Bhubaneswar?**
The total cost including extraction, implant, and crown ranges from ₹38,000 to ₹73,000.

**Can smokers get implants after extraction?**
Smoking increases the risk of implant failure. Dr. Dash recommends quitting smoking before implant placement. If you cannot quit, delayed placement with careful monitoring may be the safest option.

**What if I do not get an implant after extraction?**
If you do not replace the missing tooth, the jawbone shrinks, adjacent teeth shift into the gap, and the opposing tooth may over-erupt. This can cause bite problems and make future implant placement more difficult and expensive.

**How long after extraction can I still get an implant?**
You can get an implant years after extraction, but you may need bone grafting if the jawbone has shrunk significantly. Socket preservation at the time of extraction helps prevent this.

**Can I get an immediate implant for a molar?**
Immediate implants for molars are possible but more challenging due to the larger socket and multiple roots. Dr. Dash will assess your CBCT scan to determine if immediate placement is safe for your molar.

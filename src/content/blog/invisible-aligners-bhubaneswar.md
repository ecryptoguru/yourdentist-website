## What are invisible aligners?

Invisible aligners are clear, removable plastic trays that fit over your teeth. Each set of aligners is slightly different. Over time, they move your teeth into the desired position. You wear each set for 1 to 2 weeks before moving to the next set.

Aligners are custom-made based on digital scans or impressions of your teeth.

## How do clear aligners work?

The treatment process includes these steps:

1. **Consultation and scan:** Dr. Dash examines your teeth and takes digital scans.
2. **Treatment planning:** A digital plan maps out how your teeth will move.
3. **Aligner fabrication:** A series of aligners is made in a dental lab.
4. **Wearing aligners:** You wear each set 20 to 22 hours per day.
5. **Progress checks:** You visit the clinic every 6 to 8 weeks.
6. **Retainers:** After treatment, retainers keep teeth in position.

## Are clear aligners right for everyone?

Clear aligners work best for mild to moderate cases, such as:

- Minor crowding
- Small gaps between teeth
- Slightly crooked teeth
- Mild overbite or underbite

They are less effective for:

- Severe crowding
- Complex bite problems
- Large gaps
- Severely rotated teeth
- Jaw alignment issues

Dr. Dash evaluates each patient to determine if aligners are suitable.

## What to expect during clear aligner treatment

### First week

The aligners may feel tight for the first few days. You may have a slight lisp as your tongue adjusts. Speech usually returns to normal within a week.

### Switching aligners

Each new set feels tight for 2 to 3 days. This is normal and shows the teeth are moving. You switch to the next set as directed by your dentist.

### Eating and drinking

Remove aligners before eating or drinking anything except water. Brush your teeth before putting aligners back in to prevent cavities and staining.

### Cleaning aligners

Clean aligners daily with a soft brush and lukewarm water. Do not use hot water, which can warp the plastic.

## Retainers after clear aligners

After treatment, you need retainers to keep your teeth in position. Without retainers, teeth can shift back. Retainers may be removable or fixed behind the teeth.

## Frequently asked questions

**How much do clear aligners cost in Bhubaneswar?**

**Are clear aligners painful?**
Aligners may feel tight for a few days after each new set, but they are generally more comfortable than braces.

**How long should I wear aligners each day?**
Aligners should be worn 20 to 22 hours per day for best results.

**Can I eat with aligners on?**
No, remove aligners before eating or drinking anything except water.

**Are aligners better than braces?**
Aligners are better for mild to moderate cases and people who want a discreet option. Braces are better for complex cases.

**How often do I need to visit the dentist with aligners?**
Usually every 6 to 8 weeks for progress checks.

**What happens if I lose an aligner?**
Contact YourDentist immediately. Dr. Dash may advise you to wear the previous set while a replacement is made. Do not skip wearing aligners, as teeth can shift quickly.

**Can I drink water with aligners in?**
Yes, you can drink plain water with aligners in. Remove them for any other beverage to prevent staining and warping.

**Will aligners affect my speech?**
You may have a slight lisp for the first few days. Your tongue will adjust, and speech returns to normal within a week for most patients.

**Can I get aligners for just my top teeth?**
In some cases, single-arch treatment is possible. However, most cases require both upper and lower aligners to ensure the bite fits properly. Dr. Dash will assess if single-arch treatment is suitable for you.

**What happens after aligner treatment ends?**
You will need retainers to maintain your new smile. Dr. Dash will provide either removable or fixed retainers and advise on how long to wear them. Without retainers, teeth can shift back over time.

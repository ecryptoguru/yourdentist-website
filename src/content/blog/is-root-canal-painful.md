## Why root canal treatment is associated with pain

The fear of root canals comes from outdated methods. Decades ago, root canal treatment was slower, anesthesia was less effective, and tools were more invasive. Stories from older relatives and dramatic movie scenes have kept the myth alive. Today's root canal treatment is very different.

Modern local anesthesia can numb a tooth completely. Rotary files clean canals smoothly. Lasers disinfect without aggressive scraping. The procedure itself should not be painful.

## What causes the pain before a root canal?

The pain that sends you to the dentist is caused by infection and inflammation inside the tooth. The pulp, which contains nerves and blood vessels, becomes swollen inside the hard tooth. There is no room for swelling, so pressure builds on the nerve. This causes the severe, throbbing pain of a toothache.

A root canal removes the infected pulp, which eliminates the pressure and the pain.

## What happens during a root canal?

Understanding the procedure helps reduce fear. Here is what happens during a modern root canal at YourDentist:

1. **Numbing:** Local anesthesia is injected around the tooth. You may feel a small pinprick, then the area becomes numb.
2. **Isolation:** A rubber dam is placed around the tooth to keep it clean and dry.
3. **Access:** A small opening is made in the top of the tooth.
4. **Cleaning:** Rotary instruments remove the infected pulp and shape the canals.
5. **Disinfection:** Laser energy kills remaining bacteria.
6. **Filling:** The canals are filled with a material called gutta-percha.
7. **Sealing:** The tooth is sealed with a temporary or permanent filling.

## What does a root canal feel like?

During the procedure, you may feel:

- Pressure as instruments move inside the tooth
- A slight vibration from the rotary equipment
- The sensation of water spray used to cool the instruments

You should not feel sharp pain. If you do, we stop and add more anesthesia.

## Pain after root canal treatment

After the anesthesia wears off, mild soreness is normal for 24 to 48 hours. This is not the sharp pain of an infected tooth. It feels more like a bruise around the tooth. Normal pain medication usually manages it.

Most patients return to work the next day.

## Factors that affect pain during and after root canal

| Factor | Less discomfort | More discomfort |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Infection severity | Mild infection | Severe swelling or abscess |
| Treatment timing | Early treatment | Delayed treatment |
| Anesthesia response | Numbness works well | Difficulty getting numb |
| Tooth position | Front tooth | Molar with multiple canals |
| Post-care | Rest and soft foods | Chewing hard foods too soon |

## How to minimise pain after a root canal

- Take pain medication as advised.
- Apply a cold compress to the cheek if there is swelling.
- Eat soft foods for the first 24 hours.
- Avoid chewing on the treated side until the permanent crown is placed.
- Keep the area clean with gentle brushing.
- Attend follow-up appointments.

## Is there any alternative to root canal treatment?

The only alternative to a root canal is tooth extraction. However, extracting a tooth creates new problems. Neighbouring teeth can shift, the jawbone can shrink, and you may need an implant or bridge to replace the missing tooth. Saving the natural tooth is almost always the better option.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is root canal treatment painful?**
No. Modern root canal treatment is done under anesthesia and should not cause pain during the procedure.

**Why do root canals have a painful reputation?**
The reputation comes from older methods. Modern techniques, anesthesia, and equipment have made root canals much more comfortable.

**Will I feel pain after a root canal?**
Mild soreness for one to two days is normal. It is usually manageable with normal pain medication.

**Can a root canal be done without anesthesia?**
No. Local anesthesia is used to keep you comfortable during the procedure.

**How long does the pain last after a root canal?**
Most soreness disappears within 48 hours. If pain persists beyond a week, contact your dentist.

**What should I do if my root canal hurts?**
Take prescribed pain medication, rest, eat soft foods, and contact the clinic if the pain is severe or does not improve.

**Can I drive home after a root canal?**
Yes, root canal treatment uses local anesthesia, not sedation. You can drive yourself home after the appointment.

**How soon can I eat after a root canal?**
Wait until the anesthesia wears off (about 2 to 3 hours) to avoid biting your cheek or tongue. Eat soft foods on the first day and avoid chewing on the treated side until the crown is placed.

**Will I need antibiotics after a root canal?**
Antibiotics are not always needed. Dr. Dash may prescribe them if there was a severe infection or swelling before the procedure. Always complete the full course if prescribed.

**Can children get root canals?**
Yes, children may need root canals on baby teeth if the pulp is infected. Dr. Dash performs paediatric root canals (pulpotomy) to save baby teeth until permanent teeth erupt.

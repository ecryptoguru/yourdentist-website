## Why laser dentistry costs more than traditional methods

Laser dentistry often costs more because it requires:

- **Expensive laser equipment:** Dental lasers cost several lakhs to purchase and maintain.
- **Specialised training:** The dentist must undergo additional training to use lasers safely and effectively.
- **More precise technology:** Lasers target only diseased tissue, preserving healthy tissue.
- **Faster healing and better outcomes:** Reduced recovery time means fewer follow-up visits.
- **Lower risk of complications:** Lasers sterilise as they work, reducing infection risk.

However, the benefits of less pain, faster recovery, and better results often justify the cost. Many patients find that laser treatment saves money in the long run by preventing complications and reducing the need for repeat procedures.

## What affects the total cost of laser dental treatment

Several factors influence the final cost:

- **Number of teeth treated:** Treating one tooth costs less than full mouth treatment.
- **Severity of the condition:** Advanced gum disease costs more to treat than early-stage disease.
- **Type of laser used:** Different lasers are used for soft tissue and hard tissue procedures.
- **Additional procedures needed:** Bone grafting, crowns, or medications add to the total cost.
- **Follow-up visits:** Some conditions require multiple visits.
- **Dentist's experience:** An experienced laser dentist like Dr. Dash may charge more, but results are typically better.

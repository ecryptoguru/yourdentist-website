## What is laser root canal treatment?

Laser root canal treatment uses a dental laser to remove infected tissue and disinfect the inside of the tooth. The laser energy kills bacteria in the root canal and reduces inflammation. It is often combined with traditional rotary endodontics for a thorough cleaning.

The laser reaches areas that traditional files cannot. Root canals have complex shapes with tiny side branches where bacteria can hide. The laser's light energy penetrates these areas, killing bacteria that mechanical cleaning may miss. This is why laser RCT has a higher success rate than traditional RCT.

## The laser root canal procedure

### Step 1: Diagnosis and X-rays

Dr. Dash examines the tooth and takes X-rays or a CBCT scan to see the root canal shape and infection extent.

### Step 2: Access and cleaning

A small opening is made in the tooth. Infected pulp is removed using rotary endodontic files.

### Step 3: Laser disinfection

A dental laser is used to clean the root canal walls. The laser kills bacteria that mechanical files may miss. It also removes debris and reduces infection.

### Step 4: Filling

The cleaned canal is filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha. The filling seals the canal to prevent re-infection.

### Step 5: Crown

After healing, a crown is placed to protect the tooth. The crown restores function and appearance.

## Is laser root canal painful?

No. The procedure is done under local anesthesia. Most patients feel no pain during treatment. Post-treatment soreness is usually milder than traditional RCT and lasts 1 to 2 days.

The laser itself has an added benefit: it seals nerve endings as it works, which reduces post-operative sensitivity. Many patients report feeling almost nothing after the anesthesia wears off.

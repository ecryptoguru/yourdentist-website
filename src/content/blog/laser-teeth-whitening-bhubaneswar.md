## What is laser teeth whitening?

Laser teeth whitening is a professional whitening treatment that uses a peroxide-based gel activated by a laser or LED light. The light accelerates the whitening process, breaking down stains on the tooth surface in a single visit.

The peroxide gel penetrates the enamel and breaks down stain molecules into smaller, less visible pieces. The laser or LED light speeds up this chemical reaction, allowing dramatic results in just one session.

## Who is a good candidate?

Laser whitening works best for:

- Yellow or mild brown stains
- Discolouration from coffee, tea, wine, or smoking
- Healthy teeth and gums
- People who want fast results

Laser whitening may not work well for:

- Grey or tetracycline stains
- Stains from inside the tooth (intrinsic)
- People with tooth decay or gum disease
- Pregnant or nursing women
- Children under 16

## The first 48 hours after whitening

The first 48 hours after whitening are critical for maintaining results. Teeth are more porous and susceptible to staining during this period.

### Foods and drinks to avoid for 48 hours

- Coffee and tea
- Red wine
- Coloured soft drinks
- Berries and coloured fruits
- Tomato-based sauces
- Soy sauce and dark curries
- Tobacco and smoking

### What you can consume

- Water
- Milk
- White rice and bread
- Chicken and fish
- Plain yoghurt
- Bananas and apples

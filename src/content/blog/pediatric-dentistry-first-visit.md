## When should your child first see a dentist?

The Indian Dental Association and most paediatric dental guidelines recommend a first dental visit by the time the first tooth appears or by the child's first birthday, whichever comes first. Baby teeth usually start erupting between six and twelve months of age.

Early visits are not about treatment. They are about prevention, habit formation, and catching problems early.

## Why early dental visits matter

Baby teeth are temporary, but they are important. They help children chew, speak clearly, and guide permanent teeth into the right position. Losing baby teeth too early due to decay can cause alignment problems later.

Early visits allow the dentist to:

- Check tooth eruption and jaw development
- Identify early signs of decay
- Advise on thumb sucking, pacifier use, and oral habits
- Teach parents how to clean baby teeth
- Apply fluoride varnish if needed
- Build trust with the child

## What happens at the first dental visit?

The first visit is usually short and simple. We aim to make the child feel safe.

### Step 1: Parent discussion

We talk to the parent about feeding habits, brushing routine, fluoride use, and any concerns. We also review the child's medical history.

### Step 2: Child-friendly introduction

Dr. Arpita Dash lets the child explore the dental chair and tools in a playful way. We use simple words. We do not force anything. If the child is comfortable, we proceed. If not, we stop and try again next time.

### Step 3: Gentle examination

We examine the child's teeth, gums, and bite. We may use a small mirror and a soft probe. For very young children, the exam can often be done with the child sitting on the parent's lap.

### Step 4: Cleaning and fluoride

If the child is comfortable, we may do a gentle cleaning and apply fluoride varnish. Fluoride strengthens enamel and helps prevent cavities.

### Step 5: Oral hygiene instructions for parents

We show parents how to brush baby teeth, when to start flossing, and which foods to limit. We also discuss bottle use, nighttime feeding, and pacifier habits.

## How to prepare your child for the first dental visit

- **Start early.** Talk about the dentist as a friendly person who helps keep teeth strong.
- **Read books or watch videos** about dental visits.
- **Avoid scary words** like pain, drill, or needle.
- **Schedule at the right time.** Choose a time when your child is rested and fed, not cranky.
- **Stay calm.** Children pick up on parent anxiety.
- **Bring a comfort toy** if it helps your child relax.

## Common questions parents ask

**What if my child cries during the first visit?**
This is normal. Crying is a way for young children to express fear or discomfort. We stop, reassure, and try again. A gentle first visit is more important than doing everything at once.

**Should I clean my baby's gums?**
Yes. Before teeth erupt, wipe the gums with a clean, damp cloth after feeding. Once teeth appear, use a soft baby toothbrush.

**Can baby teeth get cavities?**
Yes. Baby bottle tooth decay can happen when a baby falls asleep with milk or juice. Never put a baby to bed with a bottle of milk, formula, or juice.

**When should my child start brushing alone?**
Children can start practicing around age three, but parents should supervise and help until around age seven or eight. Young children do not have the coordination to brush thoroughly alone.

**Is fluoride safe for children?**
Yes, in the right amount. Fluoride toothpaste should be used in a small amount, about the size of a grain of rice for children under three and a pea-sized amount for children three to six.

## Signs your child needs to see a dentist

- White or brown spots on teeth
- Complaints of tooth pain or sensitivity
- Bleeding gums when brushing
- Persistent thumb sucking after age five
- Difficulty chewing or speaking
- Early loss of baby teeth
- Teeth that do not meet properly when biting

## Tips for keeping your child's teeth healthy

1. **Limit sugary snacks and drinks.** Fruit juices, biscuits, and chocolates are common causes of cavities.
2. **Brush twice daily.** Help your child brush until they can do it well alone.
3. **Use fluoride toothpaste** in the correct amount.
4. **Avoid bottles at bedtime.** Milk and juice pool around teeth overnight.
5. **Visit the dentist every six months.** Regular checkups catch problems early.
6. **Ask about dental sealants.** Sealants protect chewing surfaces from decay.

## Frequently asked questions

**At what age should a child first visit a dentist?**
By the first birthday or within six months of the first tooth erupting, whichever comes first.

**Is the first dental visit painful for babies?**
No. The first visit is usually a gentle examination and parent counselling. No painful procedures are done unless there is an urgent problem.

**How often should children visit the dentist?**
Every six months, or more often if the dentist recommends it.

**What is baby bottle tooth decay?**
It is tooth decay caused by frequent and long exposure of teeth to milk, formula, or juice, especially during sleep.

**Do cavities in baby teeth need treatment?**
Yes. Untreated cavities in baby teeth can cause pain, infection, and problems with permanent teeth.

**How can I make brushing fun for my child?**
Use a timer, a favourite song, a reward chart, or an app. Let your child choose a toothbrush with their favourite character.

## Why might a child need a root canal?

A child may need root canal treatment, also called pulpotomy or pulpectomy in children, if:

- A deep cavity has reached the pulp
- A tooth is cracked or broken and the pulp is exposed
- There is an abscess or swelling near a baby tooth
- The tooth is painful and sensitive to hot or cold
- An injury has damaged the tooth's nerve

Baby tooth decay can progress quickly because baby enamel is thinner than adult enamel. Early treatment prevents infection from spreading.

## Is root canal treatment safe for kids?

Yes, root canal treatment for children is safe when performed by a dentist experienced with paediatric patients. The procedure is similar to adult root canal treatment but adapted for a child's smaller teeth and behaviour.

Dr. Arpita Dash uses child-friendly techniques, explains the process in simple words, and stops if the child becomes too anxious. Local anesthesia keeps the procedure painless.

## Pulpotomy vs pulpectomy in children

There are two main procedures for baby teeth:

| Procedure | What it does | When it is used |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pulpotomy | Removes infected pulp from the crown, leaving healthy root pulp | Infection limited to crown |
| Pulpectomy | Removes all pulp from crown and roots | Infection has reached roots |

A pulpotomy is less extensive and more common in children. After the procedure, the tooth is usually covered with a stainless steel crown.

## What happens during a paediatric root canal?

### Step 1: Examination and X-ray

We examine the tooth and take a small digital X-ray to see the extent of infection. We also explain the procedure to the child in age-appropriate language.

### Step 2: Numbing

A small amount of local anesthesia is given to numb the tooth and gum. We use topical numbing gel before the injection to reduce discomfort.

### Step 3: Removing infected pulp

A small opening is made in the tooth. The infected pulp is removed using gentle instruments.

### Step 4: Cleaning and medication

The inside of the tooth is cleaned. Medication may be placed to prevent infection.

### Step 5: Filling and crown

The tooth is filled with a material that resorbs naturally as the tooth falls out. A stainless steel crown is usually placed to protect the tooth.

## Why save a baby tooth instead of extracting it?

Baby teeth are temporary, but they have important jobs:

- Help children chew food properly
- Support speech development
- Hold space for permanent teeth
- Guide permanent teeth into correct position
- Maintain jawbone structure

If a baby tooth is lost too early, neighbouring teeth can shift and block the permanent tooth from erupting properly. This may lead to orthodontic problems later.

## Signs your child may need a root canal

Parents should watch for these signs:

- Complaints of tooth pain, especially at night
- Sensitivity to hot or cold foods
- Swelling or a pimple on the gum
- Discoloured tooth
- Difficulty eating or chewing
- Fever with tooth pain
- Bad breath that does not go away

If you notice any of these, book a dental checkup.

## Is the procedure painful for children?

With modern anesthesia, the procedure should not be painful. Some children feel pressure or vibration. After the anesthesia wears off, mild soreness is normal for 24 to 48 hours. This can be managed with child-appropriate pain medication.

## Aftercare for children

After a paediatric root canal:

- Give soft foods for the first day.
- Avoid letting the child chew hard or sticky foods on the treated side.
- Use a cold compress on the cheek if there is swelling.
- Give pain medication as directed by the dentist.
- Keep the area clean with gentle brushing.
- Attend the follow-up appointment.

## Paediatric root canal vs extraction: comparison

| Factor | Root canal (pulpotomy/pulpectomy) | Extraction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Saves tooth | Yes | No |
| Space maintenance | Yes, tooth holds space | Space may close, causing crowding |
| Chewing function | Preserved | Lost on that side |
| Speech development | Supported | May be affected |
| Need for space maintainer | No | Yes, to prevent shifting |
| Cost | ₹1,500 - ₹6,000 | ₹500 - ₹2,000 + space maintainer |

## Frequently asked questions

**Is root canal safe for children?**
Yes, paediatric root canal treatment is safe and commonly performed to save infected baby teeth.

**Why save a baby tooth if it will fall out anyway?**
Baby teeth hold space for permanent teeth and help with chewing and speech. Losing them early can cause alignment problems.

**How long does a paediatric root canal take?**
The procedure usually takes 30 to 60 minutes depending on the tooth.

**Will my child feel pain during the procedure?**
No, local anesthesia keeps the procedure painless. Mild soreness after is normal.

**What is the difference between pulpotomy and pulpectomy?**
A pulpotomy removes infected pulp from the crown. A pulpectomy removes all pulp from the crown and roots.

**How can I prevent cavities in my child's teeth?**
Limit sugar, brush twice daily, use fluoride toothpaste, and visit the dentist regularly.

**Will my child need a crown after a root canal?**
Yes, most baby teeth need a crown after root canal treatment to protect them from fracture. Stainless steel crowns are most common for back teeth. Tooth-coloured crowns are available for front teeth.

**Can my child go to school the next day after a root canal?**
Yes, most children return to school the next day. Mild soreness may persist but is manageable with child-safe pain medication. Avoid giving hard or sticky foods for a day or two.

**How do I prepare my child for a dental procedure?**
Stay positive and calm. Use simple, non-scary words like "the dentist will clean your tooth and make it healthy." Avoid saying "it won't hurt" as this introduces the concept of pain. Dr. Dash is experienced in making children feel comfortable and safe.

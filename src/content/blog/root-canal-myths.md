This article addresses five common root canal myths that I hear from patients at YourDentist Laser Dental Clinic.

## Myth 1: Root canal treatment is very painful

This is the most common myth, and it was probably true decades ago. Older root canal procedures used less effective anesthesia, rigid steel files, and multiple long visits. Modern root canal treatment is a different experience.

Today, we use strong local anesthesia, flexible rotary files, and laser-assisted disinfection. Most patients at our Bhubaneswar clinic say the procedure feels like getting a filling. The pain that people remember usually comes from the infected tooth before treatment, not from the treatment itself. A root canal removes the source of that pain.

## Myth 2: Extraction is better than root canal treatment

Some patients ask, "Why not just remove the tooth?" Extraction seems cheaper and faster, but it creates long-term problems. A missing tooth causes neighbouring teeth to shift, affects your bite, and can lead to bone loss in the jaw. Replacing the tooth with an implant or bridge costs more than a root canal and crown.

Saving your natural tooth with root canal treatment is almost always the better option if the tooth can be restored. Your natural tooth helps preserve bone structure, maintains your bite, and looks better than most replacements.

## Myth 3: Root canal treatment causes illness

This myth comes from a poorly conducted study from the early 1900s that suggested bacteria trapped in root canals could cause diseases like arthritis and heart disease. The research has been discredited, and no modern study has proven this link.

When a root canal is done correctly, the infected pulp is removed, the canals are cleaned and sealed, and bacteria are eliminated. Root canal treatment does not cause illness. Leaving an infected tooth untreated is the real risk, because the infection can spread to other parts of the body.

## Myth 4: Root canals always need multiple visits

Many patients expect root canal treatment to take two or three visits. In reality, most cases at YourDentist are completed in a single sitting. We use rotary endodontics and laser disinfection to clean and fill the canals in one appointment.

Multiple visits are only needed in severe cases where there is swelling, pus drainage, or a complex infection. In those situations, we place medication inside the tooth for a few days and complete the filling later.

## Myth 5: A root canal removes the whole tooth

Some people think a root canal means the tooth is taken out. It does not. Root canal treatment removes only the infected pulp inside the tooth. The outer tooth structure stays in place and is restored with a filling or crown.

Think of the pulp as the soft core of the tooth. Removing it does not make the tooth weak by itself, but the tooth does need a crown afterwards to protect it from cracking under chewing pressure.

## When do you actually need a root canal?

You may need root canal treatment if you have:

- Severe toothache, especially when chewing or at night
- Sensitivity to hot or cold that lasts more than a few seconds
- Darkening or discolouration of a single tooth
- Swelling or tenderness in the gums near a tooth
- A recurring pimple or bump on the gum
- Deep decay that has reached the pulp

If you notice any of these signs, see a dentist as soon as possible. Early treatment is simpler and more comfortable.

## What to expect after root canal treatment

Mild soreness for a day or two is normal. It is not the sharp pain of an infected tooth. Most patients manage it with normal pain relievers and return to work the next day. You should eat soft foods for the first day and avoid chewing on the treated side until your permanent crown is placed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is root canal treatment safe?**
Yes. Modern root canal treatment is a safe and routine procedure. It removes infection and saves your natural tooth.

**Does root canal treatment cause other diseases?**
No. This claim is based on outdated and discredited research. Root canal treatment eliminates bacteria from the infected tooth.

**How painful is a root canal really?**
With modern anesthesia, you should not feel pain during the procedure. Some pressure is normal. Mild soreness after treatment lasts a day or two.

**Can a root canal be done in one visit?**
Yes, most cases at our Bhubaneswar clinic are completed in a single sitting.

**What happens if I delay root canal treatment?**
Delaying treatment can lead to abscess, bone infection, and possible tooth loss. The infection can also spread to other areas.

**Do I need a crown after every root canal?**
Most teeth need a crown after RCT for protection. Front teeth with minimal damage may sometimes be restored with a filling.

**How successful is root canal treatment?**
Root canal treatment has a success rate of over 95% when performed by an experienced dentist using modern technology. At YourDentist, Dr. Dash uses rotary endodontics and laser disinfection to achieve consistently high success rates.

**Can a root canal tooth get infected again?**
Reinfection is rare but possible if the tooth is not properly sealed or if new decay develops. Regular checkups and good oral hygiene prevent reinfection. If reinfection occurs, retreatment can usually save the tooth.

**Is root canal treatment safe during pregnancy?**
Yes, root canal treatment can be performed during the second trimester if necessary. Dr. Dash takes precautions including using pregnancy-safe anesthesia and avoiding X-rays unless absolutely needed with proper shielding.

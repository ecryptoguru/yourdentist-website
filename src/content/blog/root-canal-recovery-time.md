## What happens immediately after a root canal?

After the procedure, your mouth will remain numb for 1 to 3 hours due to local anesthesia. You should avoid eating or drinking hot liquids during this time to prevent biting your cheek or tongue. Once the numbness wears off, you may feel mild soreness around the treated tooth and jaw.

This soreness is not the sharp pain of the original infection. It feels more like tenderness or a bruise.

## Typical root canal recovery timeline

| Time | What to expect |
| --- | --- |
| First 2 hours | Numbness from anesthesia |
| First 24 hours | Mild soreness, manageable with pain medication |
| 2 to 3 days | Soreness decreases significantly |
| 1 week | Most patients feel normal |
| 2 to 4 weeks | Permanent crown placed, full function restored |

## When can you return to work after a root canal?

Most patients can return to work the same day or the next day after a root canal. If your job is physically demanding or involves a lot of talking, you may prefer to take one day off.

| Job type | Recommended return time |
| --- | --- |
| Desk work | Same day or next day |
| Teaching or public speaking | Next day |
| Manual labour | 1 to 2 days |
| Sports or fitness training | 2 to 3 days |

## Can you drive after a root canal?

Yes, if you only had local anesthesia. It is safe to drive once the numbness wears off and you feel alert. If you received any sedation, arrange for someone to drive you home.

## Returning to exercise after a root canal

Light exercise like walking can be done the next day. Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 to 48 hours because increased blood flow can worsen swelling or bleeding from the gum. If your root canal was complicated or involved an abscess, wait 3 to 5 days before heavy workouts.

## Eating after a root canal

Wait until the anesthesia wears off before eating. Start with soft foods for the first 24 hours. Chew on the opposite side of the treated tooth. Avoid hot, hard, crunchy, and sticky foods until the permanent crown is placed.

## Managing pain after root canal treatment

Most patients do not need strong pain medication. Over-the-counter pain relievers are usually enough. Take medication as directed by your dentist. If pain increases after 3 days or does not improve, contact the clinic.

## Swelling after a root canal

Mild swelling of the gum near the treated tooth is normal. It should peak within 24 hours and then improve. Apply a cold compress to the cheek for 15 minutes at a time to reduce swelling. If swelling increases after 3 days or is severe, call the clinic.

## Signs of a normal recovery

- Mild soreness that improves each day
- Slight tenderness when chewing
- No fever
- Swelling that decreases after 2 to 3 days
- Ability to eat soft foods

## Signs that you should contact your dentist

- Severe pain that worsens after 2 to 3 days
- Swelling that increases or spreads
- Fever
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing
- Foul taste or smell from the tooth
- Temporary filling or crown falls out

These symptoms could indicate infection or a complication that needs attention.

## How to speed up root canal recovery

1. **Take rest for the first day.** Avoid unnecessary physical strain.
2. **Use pain medication as advised.** Do not skip doses if you are sore.
3. **Apply cold compresses.** This reduces swelling in the first 24 hours.
4. **Eat soft foods.** Give your tooth and jaw a break.
5. **Keep your mouth clean.** Brush gently and rinse with warm salt water.
6. **Avoid smoking.** Smoking slows healing and increases infection risk.
7. **Attend follow-up appointments.** Do not skip the crown placement visit.

## Root canal recovery vs extraction recovery

Root canal recovery is generally faster and more comfortable than extraction recovery. After a root canal, the tooth stays in place. After extraction, the socket needs to heal, and there is more bleeding and swelling risk.

| Factor | Root canal recovery | Extraction recovery |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Return to work | Next day | 2 to 3 days |
| Soreness | Mild, 1 to 3 days | Moderate, 3 to 7 days |
| Swelling | Minimal | More common |
| Bleeding | None | Common first day |
| Diet restriction | 2 to 3 days soft food | 3 to 7 days soft food |

## Permanent crown placement

The final step in root canal recovery is getting a permanent crown. This usually happens 1 to 2 weeks after the root canal. The crown protects the tooth and allows you to chew normally. Without a crown, the treated tooth can crack.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does it take to recover from a root canal?**
Most patients recover within 2 to 3 days. Full function is restored after the permanent crown is placed.

**Can I go back to work the day after a root canal?**
Yes, most people return to work the next day.

**How long does swelling last after a root canal?**
Mild swelling usually peaks within 24 hours and improves over 2 to 3 days.

**Can I exercise after a root canal?**
Light exercise is fine after 24 hours. Avoid strenuous exercise for 2 to 3 days.

**When can I eat normally after a root canal?**
You can eat soft foods immediately. Normal eating resumes gradually over 1 to 2 weeks.

**What if my tooth still hurts a week after a root canal?**
Mild soreness is normal for a few days. If pain persists beyond a week, contact your dentist.

**Can I drink coffee after a root canal?**
Avoid hot drinks for the first 24 hours while you are still numb. After that, you can drink coffee, but avoid very hot temperatures for 2 to 3 days. Do not use a straw for the first 24 hours.

**Will I need antibiotics after a root canal?**
Antibiotics are not always needed after a root canal. Dr. Dash may prescribe them if there was a severe infection, swelling, or pus. Always complete the full course if prescribed.

**Can I fly after a root canal?**
It is best to wait 24 to 48 hours after a root canal before flying. Changes in cabin pressure can cause discomfort in a recently treated tooth. If you must travel, consult Dr. Dash first.

**How soon can I brush the treated tooth?**
You can brush normally the same day, but be gentle around the treated tooth. Avoid vigorous rinsing for 24 hours. Use a soft-bristle brush.

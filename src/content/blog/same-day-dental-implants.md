Same-day dental implants, also called immediate implants, place the implant and a temporary tooth in a single visit. This means you walk out of the clinic with a replacement tooth the same day your natural tooth is removed, or the same day your implant is placed. Immediate implants have a success rate of approximately 90 to 95% when case selection is done properly.

In Bhubaneswar, many patients are drawn to same-day implants because they want to avoid having a visible gap in their smile. This is especially important for front teeth and for professionals who interact with people regularly. However, same-day implants are not suitable for everyone and require careful evaluation.

## What are same-day dental implants?

Traditional implant treatment takes 3 to 6 months. The implant is placed, the gum is closed, and you wait for healing before the crown is attached. Same-day implants skip the waiting period by placing a temporary crown on the implant immediately.

## Who is a candidate for same-day implants?

Same-day implants are not for everyone. Dr. Dash evaluates the following:

### Good candidates

- Healthy gums with no infection
- Sufficient jawbone density and volume
- Good oral hygiene
- Non-smoker
- No uncontrolled medical conditions
- Front tooth replacement where aesthetics matter
- Stable implant at the time of placement

### Not suitable for

- Active gum disease
- Severe bone loss requiring grafting
- Heavy smokers
- Uncontrolled diabetes
- Teeth with severe infection at the root
- Patients who grind teeth heavily

## The same-day implant procedure

### Step 1: Consultation and CBCT scan

Dr. Dash examines the tooth and takes a CBCT scan to assess bone quality and plan the implant position.

### Step 2: Tooth extraction (if needed)

If a tooth needs to be removed, it is extracted carefully to preserve the surrounding bone.

### Step 3: Implant placement

The implant is placed into the socket immediately after extraction. The implant must be stable enough to support a temporary crown.

### Step 4: Temporary crown

A temporary crown is attached to the implant. This crown is not for chewing hard foods. It is mainly for appearance and gum shaping.

### Step 5: Healing

Over the next 2 to 6 months, the implant fuses with the bone. You wear the temporary crown during this time.

### Step 6: Permanent crown

Once healing is complete, the temporary crown is replaced with a permanent crown.

## Risks of same-day implants

Same-day implants have a slightly higher risk of failure than traditional implants because the implant is loaded immediately. Risks include:

- Imant not fusing with bone
- Temporary crown coming loose
- Gum recession around the implant
- Infection

Dr. Dash minimises these risks through careful case selection and CBCT-guided planning.

## What to expect during healing

- The temporary crown is not for chewing. Avoid biting on it.
- Eat soft foods on the opposite side for 2 to 4 weeks.
- Brush gently around the implant.
- Do not smoke.
- Attend all follow-up appointments.

## Caring for a same-day implant

- Brush twice daily with a soft brush
- Do not chew hard foods on the implant side for 6 to 8 weeks
- Use a chlorhexidine mouthwash as directed
- Visit the dentist for regular checkups
- Wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth
- Avoid smoking during the entire healing period
- Report any looseness of the temporary crown immediately

**Can I get a dental implant the same day as extraction?**

**How much does a same-day implant cost in Bhubaneswar?**

**Is same-day implant safe?**
Yes, when done by an experienced dentist on a suitable candidate. The success rate is slightly lower than traditional implants but still high.

**Can I eat with the temporary crown?**
The temporary crown is for appearance only. Avoid chewing on it for 6 to 8 weeks.

**How long until I get my permanent crown?**
The permanent crown is placed after 3 to 6 months of healing.

**Can smokers get same-day implants?**
Smoking significantly increases the risk of implant failure. Dr. Dash recommends quitting smoking at least 2 weeks before and during the entire healing period for same-day implants.

**What happens if the implant fails?**
If an implant fails to fuse with the bone, it is removed and the area is allowed to heal. A new implant can be placed after 3 to 6 months. Dr. Dash monitors healing closely to detect any issues early.

**Are same-day implants more expensive than traditional implants?**
Same-day implants cost slightly more due to the immediate temporary crown and more complex planning, but the difference is usually modest.

**Can I get same-day implants for multiple teeth?**
Yes, in some cases multiple teeth can be replaced with same-day implants. Dr. Dash will assess whether you are a suitable candidate based on bone quality and overall oral health.

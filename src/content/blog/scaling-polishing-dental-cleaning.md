## What is scaling and polishing?

Scaling is the process of removing tartar (hardened plaque) from tooth surfaces and below the gumline. Polishing smooths the tooth surface, removing stains and making it harder for plaque to stick.

## Difference between scaling, polishing, and deep cleaning

| Procedure | What it does | When needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scaling | Removes tartar above and below gumline | Routine cleaning every 6 months |
| Polishing | Removes stains and smoothens tooth surface | After scaling |
| Deep cleaning (root planing) | Cleans root surfaces under gums | Gum disease with deep pockets |

## Why scaling and polishing is necessary

### Removes tartar

Tartar is hardened plaque that brushing cannot remove. It irritates gums and causes gum disease.

### Prevents gum disease

Regular scaling prevents gingivitis from progressing to periodontitis, which can lead to tooth loss.

### Removes stains

Polishing removes surface stains from coffee, tea, tobacco, and food, making teeth look brighter.

### Prevents bad breath

Bacteria in tartar produce foul-smelling gases. Scaling reduces bad breath.

### Protects overall health

Gum disease has been linked to heart disease, diabetes, and respiratory problems. Keeping gums healthy supports overall health.

## The scaling and polishing procedure

### Step 1: Examination

Dr. Dash examines your teeth and gums. X-rays may be taken if gum disease is suspected.

### Step 2: Scaling

An ultrasonic scaler vibrates to break tartar into small pieces. Water flushes away debris. For deep pockets, hand scalers may be used below the gumline.

### Step 3: Polishing

A polishing paste is applied to the teeth with a rotating rubber cup. This removes surface stains and smoothens enamel.

### Step 4: Fluoride treatment

Fluoride may be applied to strengthen enamel and reduce sensitivity after cleaning.

## Does scaling damage teeth?

No. Scaling does not damage enamel when done correctly. The ultrasonic scaler only removes tartar, not healthy tooth structure. Some patients feel mild sensitivity for 1 to 2 days after cleaning, but this is temporary.

## How often should you get scaling and polishing?

Most people should have professional cleaning every 6 months. People with gum disease may need cleaning every 3 to 4 months.

## Signs you need scaling and polishing

- Yellow or brown tartar near gumline
- Bleeding gums when brushing
- Persistent bad breath
- Receding gums
- Sensitive teeth
- Stained teeth
- It has been more than 6 months since your last cleaning

## Aftercare tips after scaling

- Avoid very hot or cold foods for 24 hours if you feel sensitivity
- Do not smoke for 24 hours
- Brush gently with a soft brush
- Use fluoride toothpaste
- Rinse with warm salt water
- Avoid staining foods for 24 to 48 hours

## Scaling and polishing vs whitening

Scaling and polishing removes surface stains and makes teeth look cleaner, but it does not change the natural colour of teeth. Whitening uses bleaching agents to lighten tooth colour from within.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is scaling painful?**
Routine scaling is usually painless. Some patients feel mild vibration or sensitivity. Deep cleaning may need local anaesthesia.

**How long does scaling and polishing take?**
A routine cleaning takes 30 to 45 minutes. Deep cleaning may take 60 to 90 minutes per quadrant.

**Does scaling whiten teeth?**
Scaling removes surface stains and makes teeth look cleaner, but it does not bleach teeth like whitening.

**How often should I get scaling done?**
Every 6 months for most people. Every 3 to 4 months for people with gum disease.

**Can scaling loosen teeth?**
No. Scaling removes tartar that may make teeth feel tight. Healthy teeth become stronger after cleaning.

**Can I eat normally after scaling?**
Yes, but avoid very hot, cold, or sticky foods for 24 hours if you experience sensitivity. Stick to soft foods and rinse with warm salt water.

**Is laser cleaning better than ultrasonic scaling?**
Laser cleaning is used for gum disease treatment, not routine cleaning. Ultrasonic scaling is the standard for routine tartar removal. Dr. Dash may combine both for patients with gum disease.

**Does dental insurance cover scaling and polishing?**
Many dental insurance plans cover one or two professional cleanings per year. Check with your provider for details.

**What is the difference between cleaning and deep cleaning?**
Routine cleaning removes tartar above the gumline. Deep cleaning (root planing) cleans below the gumline and is needed when gum disease has created deep pockets around teeth.

**Can I drink tea or coffee after scaling?**
Avoid staining foods and drinks like tea, coffee, and turmeric-based foods for 24 to 48 hours after polishing. The freshly polished tooth surface is more susceptible to staining during this period.

**Will my teeth be sensitive after scaling?**
Mild sensitivity to hot and cold is normal for 1 to 2 days after scaling. This is temporary and resolves on its own. Using fluoride toothpaste and avoiding extreme temperatures helps. If sensitivity persists beyond a week, contact Dr. Dash.

**Can pregnant women get scaling done?**
Yes, professional cleaning is safe during pregnancy and is actually recommended. Pregnancy hormones increase the risk of gum inflammation (pregnancy gingivitis). The second trimester is the safest time for dental cleaning. Inform Dr. Dash about your pregnancy before treatment.

Root canal treatment is one of the most feared dental procedures, but the symptoms that lead to it are far worse than the treatment itself. If you recognise the warning signs early, you can save your tooth and avoid severe infection.

## 1. Severe tooth pain, especially when chewing or at night

A tooth that hurts when you bite down or throbs at night is one of the strongest signs of pulp infection. The pain may come and go at first, but it usually gets worse over time. Nighttime pain happens because lying flat increases blood pressure around the infected tooth.

If pain medication only helps for a short while, the problem is inside the tooth and needs professional treatment.

## 2. Sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers

Many people have mild sensitivity to ice cream or hot tea. That is normal and usually lasts only a few seconds. The problem is when the pain lingers for 30 seconds or more after the hot or cold stimulus is gone.

Lingering sensitivity means the nerve inside the tooth is inflamed. This is a common early sign that root canal treatment may be needed.

## 3. Darkening or discolouration of a single tooth

If one tooth becomes darker than the others, it may be dying. Trauma, deep decay, or a crack can damage the blood vessels inside the tooth. The tooth may turn grey, brown, or yellow compared to surrounding teeth.

Discolouration alone does not always mean you need a root canal, but it means the tooth needs examination.

## 4. Swelling and tenderness in the gums

An infected tooth can cause swelling in the gum tissue around it. The gum may look red, feel puffy, and be tender to touch. Sometimes the swelling spreads to the cheek or jaw.

Swelling is a sign that infection has moved beyond the tooth into the surrounding tissue. This needs prompt dental care.

## 5. A recurring pimple or bump on the gum

A small bump on the gum near a tooth is called a dental abscess or fistula. It may look like a pimple and may drain fluid. It can come and go, but it means there is an active infection at the root tip.

Ignoring this bump can allow the infection to damage the jawbone and spread to other areas.

## 6. Cracked or chipped tooth with deep pain

A cracked tooth can expose the pulp to bacteria. If the crack reaches the nerve, the tooth may need root canal treatment. Deep pain when releasing pressure after biting is a classic sign of a cracked tooth.

Even small cracks can lead to infection if bacteria get inside. A crown is usually needed after treatment to protect the tooth.

## 7. Prolonged pain after a filling or crown

Sometimes a tooth that recently had a deep filling or crown preparation becomes sensitive. If the sensitivity does not improve within a few weeks, the pulp may be irreversibly inflamed. The tooth may need root canal treatment before the permanent restoration is placed.

## What causes these symptoms?

The pulp inside your tooth contains nerves and blood vessels. When bacteria reach the pulp through deep decay, cracks, repeated dental procedures, or trauma, the pulp becomes inflamed and infected. As pressure builds inside the hard tooth, it compresses the nerve and causes pain.

If the infection is not treated, it can spread through the root tip into the bone and form an abscess.

Yes, in most cases. Root canal treatment removes the infected pulp and seals the tooth. A crown placed afterward protects the tooth and restores function. This is almost always better than extraction, which leads to bone loss and shifting of neighbouring teeth.

Delaying root canal treatment can lead to:

- More severe pain and swelling
- Spread of infection to the jawbone
- Abscess formation
- Possible tooth loss
- Higher treatment costs later

A root canal done early is usually simpler, more comfortable, and more successful.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a tooth infection heal on its own?**
No. The infection is inside the tooth where your immune system cannot reach it. It needs root canal treatment or extraction.

**How do I know if my toothache is serious?**
If the pain is severe, lasts more than a day or two, or is accompanied by swelling or fever, see a dentist immediately.

**Is a root canal the only option for an infected tooth?**
Root canal treatment or extraction are the two options. Saving the tooth with a root canal is usually preferred.

**How urgent is a root canal?**
Urgent. Delaying treatment can lead to abscess, bone infection, and spread of bacteria.

**Can I take antibiotics instead of a root canal?**
Antibiotics may reduce symptoms temporarily, but they do not remove the infected pulp. The infection will return.

**What happens if I ignore the symptoms?**
The infection can spread, cause severe pain, damage bone, and may require extraction or hospital treatment in extreme cases.

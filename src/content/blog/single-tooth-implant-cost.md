## What is a single tooth implant?

A single tooth implant replaces one missing tooth. It has three parts:

| Part | Description | Lifespan |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Implant fixture | Titanium post placed in jawbone | 15 to 25+ years |
| Abutment | Connector between implant and crown | 15 to 25+ years |
| Crown | Artificial tooth visible above gum | 10 to 15 years |

Unlike a dental bridge, an implant does not require grinding down adjacent teeth. It also preserves jawbone and prevents bone loss.

## Factors that affect single implant cost

### Implant brand

Premium brands from Europe, the USA, or South Korea cost more but offer better long-term reliability and warranty. Indian brands are more affordable but may not have the same track record.

### Crown material

Metal-ceramic crowns are more affordable. Zirconia crowns cost more but look more natural and are more durable. For front teeth, zirconia is usually recommended.

### Bone grafting

If the jawbone under the missing tooth has shrunk, bone grafting may be needed before implant placement. This adds ₹5,000 to ₹20,000 to the total cost.

### Tooth position

Front teeth implants may cost slightly more because the crown needs to look highly aesthetic. Molar implants need stronger components for chewing forces.

### Dentist experience

An experienced implant dentist like Dr. Arpita Dash may charge more, but the success rate and long-term outcome are better.

Implant surgery is done under local anaesthesia, so you should not feel pain during the procedure. Mild swelling and discomfort for 2 to 3 days after surgery is normal and manageable with pain medication.

Smoking increases the risk of implant failure by reducing blood flow to the gums. Dr. Dash recommends quitting or reducing smoking before and after implant surgery for the best results.

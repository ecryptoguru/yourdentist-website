## Laser teeth whitening

Laser whitening is the fastest and most effective method. A whitening gel is applied to the teeth and activated with a laser or LED light. The gel breaks down stains and lightens the tooth colour.

### Advantages

- Fast results in a single session
- Can lighten teeth by 5 to 8 shades
- Performed by a dentist for safety
- Immediate visible results

### Disadvantages

- More expensive than chemical whitening
- May cause temporary sensitivity
- Not suitable for all types of discolouration

## Chemical teeth whitening

Chemical whitening uses a peroxide-based gel applied to the teeth with custom trays. The gel stays on the teeth for a set period, usually 30 to 45 minutes per session.

### Advantages of chemical whitening

- More affordable than laser
- Gentler on sensitive teeth
- Can be done partially at home with take-home trays
- Gradual, natural-looking results

### Disadvantages of chemical whitening

- Takes longer to see results
- Requires multiple visits or weeks of home use
- Results may not be as dramatic as laser

## What causes tooth discolouration?

### Extrinsic stains (surface)

- Coffee, tea, and red wine
- Tobacco and smoking
- Coloured foods like berries and curry
- Poor oral hygiene

### Intrinsic stains (inside the tooth)

- Aging (enamel thins, dentin shows through)
- Trauma or injury to the tooth
- Excessive fluoride during childhood
- Certain medications (tetracycline)
- Root canal treatment

Whitening works best on extrinsic stains. Intrinsic stains may need veneers or crowns.

## The whitening procedure at YourDentist

### Laser whitening

1. **Cleaning:** Teeth are cleaned and polished.
2. **Gum protection:** A barrier is placed to protect the gums.
3. **Gel application:** Whitening gel is applied to the teeth.
4. **Laser activation:** A laser activates the gel for 15 to 20 minutes.
5. **Repeat:** The gel may be reapplied 2 to 3 times in the same session.
6. **Final check:** Results are evaluated and aftercare instructions given.

### Chemical whitening

1. **Cleaning:** Teeth are cleaned and polished.
2. **Tray fitting:** Custom trays are made from impressions.
3. **Gel application:** Whitening gel is placed in the trays.
4. **Wearing:** Trays are worn for 30 to 45 minutes per session.
5. **Repeat:** 2 to 3 sessions for desired results.

## Who should not get teeth whitening?

- Pregnant or nursing women
- Children under 16
- People with severe tooth sensitivity
- People with gum disease
- People with cavities (need treatment first)
- People with crowns or veneers on front teeth (these do not whiten)

## Frequently asked questions

**How much does teeth whitening cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Laser whitening costs ₹8,000 to ₹12,000. Chemical whitening costs ₹5,000 to ₹8,000 at YourDentist.

**Is laser whitening better than chemical whitening?**
Laser whitening is faster and can achieve more shades of improvement in one session. Chemical whitening is gentler and more affordable.

**How long does teeth whitening last?**
Results last 1 to 3 years depending on your diet, oral hygiene, and whether you smoke.

**Does teeth whitening damage teeth?**
No. Professional whitening by a dentist is safe and does not damage enamel when done correctly.

**Can I whiten crowns or veneers?**
No. Crowns and veneers do not respond to whitening. If they are stained, they need to be replaced.

**Does teeth whitening cause sensitivity?**
Some patients experience temporary sensitivity for 1 to 3 days after whitening. This is normal and resolves on its own. Dr. Dash can apply a desensitising agent if needed.

**Can I eat normally after teeth whitening?**
For the first 48 hours, avoid staining foods and drinks like coffee, tea, red wine, and coloured curries. Stick to a white diet (rice, milk, yogurt, chicken, bread) for best results.

**How often can I whiten my teeth?**
Professional whitening can be done every 6 to 12 months. Over-whitening can damage enamel, so it is important to follow Dr. Dash's recommendations.

**Are over-the-counter whitening kits safe?**
Over-the-counter kits are less effective and can damage gums if not used correctly. Professional whitening at a dental clinic uses controlled gel concentrations and gum protection for safe, predictable results.

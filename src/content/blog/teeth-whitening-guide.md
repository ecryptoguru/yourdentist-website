## Why teeth become yellow or stained

Teeth change colour for many reasons. Some are within your control, and some are not.

### Surface stains

Foods and drinks like coffee, tea, red wine, cola, and dark berries leave pigments on the enamel. Tobacco is one of the strongest staining agents. These surface stains usually respond well to professional whitening.

### Internal discolouration

Some stains come from inside the tooth. This can happen due to aging, trauma, excessive fluoride during childhood, or certain antibiotics like tetracycline. Internal stains are harder to treat and may need veneers or bonding instead of whitening.

### Enamel thinning

As enamel wears down over time, the yellow dentin underneath becomes more visible. This is a normal part of aging, but whitening can still help in many cases.

## In-office teeth whitening procedure at YourDentist

Our professional whitening procedure is safe, controlled, and delivers visible results in about an hour.

### Step 1: Examination and shade matching

We check your teeth and gums to make sure whitening is suitable. We note any cavities, gum recession, or restorations that need attention first. Then we take a shade photo so you can compare before and after.

### Step 2: Cleaning

A professional cleaning removes surface plaque and tartar. This allows the whitening gel to work evenly across your teeth.

### Step 3: Gum protection

We apply a protective barrier to your gums and lips. This prevents the whitening gel from irritating soft tissue.

### Step 4: Whitening gel application

A professional whitening gel, usually containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, is applied to the teeth.

### Step 5: Activation

For laser whitening, a special light or laser activates the gel and speeds up the whitening process. This is done in 15 to 20 minute cycles, repeated two or three times depending on your target shade.

### Step 6: Final rinse and review

After the final cycle, the gel is removed and your teeth are rinsed. We take an after photo and compare the shade. Most patients see a difference of four to eight shades in one session.

## How long does teeth whitening take?

In-office whitening takes about 60 to 90 minutes. Take-home trays require wearing them 30 to 60 minutes per day for one to two weeks. Results appear gradually with trays and immediately with in-office treatment.

## How long does teeth whitening last?

Professional whitening results can last one to three years, depending on your habits. Avoiding coffee, tea, red wine, tobacco, and dark-coloured foods will help results last longer. Occasional touch-ups with take-home trays can keep your smile bright.

## Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, when performed by a dentist. The most common side effect is temporary sensitivity, which usually goes away within 24 to 48 hours. We use desensitising agents and customise the concentration based on your sensitivity level.

Whitening toothpastes are safe for short-term use but should not replace professional care. Over-the-counter strips and online gels can cause uneven results or damage enamel if misused.

## Teeth whitening vs veneers

Whitening changes the colour of natural enamel. Veneers cover the front surface of the tooth with a thin layer of porcelain or composite. If your teeth are severely stained, chipped, or misshapen, veneers may give a better overall result.

## How to maintain white teeth after treatment

1. Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste.
2. Rinse or drink water after consuming staining foods and drinks.
3. Use a straw for dark-coloured beverages.
4. Avoid tobacco completely.
5. Schedule regular cleanings every six months.
6. Use touch-up trays as directed by your dentist.

## Who should avoid teeth whitening?

Teeth whitening is not recommended for:

- Children under 16
- Pregnant or breastfeeding women
- People with untreated cavities or gum disease
- Patients with hypersensitive teeth
- People with allergies to whitening ingredients
- Individuals with visible restorations that will not whiten

If you have crowns, fillings, or veneers on front teeth, they will not change colour with whitening. We may recommend replacing them after whitening to match your new shade.

## Frequently asked questions

**How much does teeth whitening cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Professional teeth whitening at YourDentist ranges from ₹5,000 to ₹12,000 depending on the method.

**Is laser teeth whitening better than chemical whitening?**
Laser whitening works faster and can lift more shades in one session. Chemical whitening is gentler and better for sensitive teeth.

**How long does teeth whitening last?**
Results last one to three years with good care. Touch-up treatments can extend this.

**Does teeth whitening damage enamel?**
Professional whitening does not damage enamel when done correctly. Overuse of home kits or abrasive toothpastes can cause harm.

**Can yellow teeth become white again?**
Most surface yellowing can be improved. Deep internal stains may need veneers or bonding.

**Can I whiten my teeth if I have cavities?**
No, cavities should be treated first. Whitening gel can reach the inner tooth through cavities and cause pain.

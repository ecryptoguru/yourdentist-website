## Why clean your tongue?

The tongue is covered with bacteria. When these bacteria break down food particles and dead cells, they release sulphur compounds that cause bad breath. Cleaning the tongue removes this coating and improves oral health.

### Reduces bad breath

A coated tongue is one of the leading causes of halitosis. Scraping the tongue removes the bacteria responsible for foul odour.

### Improves taste

Removing the coating from your tongue can improve your sense of taste. Food flavours become clearer.

### Reduces plaque

Bacteria on the tongue can transfer to teeth and gums, increasing plaque and the risk of cavities and gum disease.

### Improves appearance

A white or yellow-coated tongue looks unhealthy. Regular cleaning keeps the tongue pink and healthy.

## Tongue cleaning tools

| Tool | Description | Best for |

| Spoon | Can be used gently | Temporary solution |
| Tongue brush | Soft bristles | Some people prefer brushes |

A tongue scraper is more effective than a toothbrush because it removes the coating more thoroughly.

## How to clean your tongue

### Step 1: Use a tongue scraper

Hold the scraper at the back of your tongue. Use gentle pressure.

### Step 2: Scrape forward

Pull the scraper forward from the back to the tip of the tongue. Do not press too hard.

### Step 3: Rinse the scraper

Rinse the scraper after each pass to remove debris.

### Step 4: Repeat

Repeat 5 to 10 times until the tongue looks clean.

### Step 5: Rinse your mouth

Rinse with water or mouthwash after cleaning.

## How far back should you scrape?

Clean as far back as comfortable without gagging. The back of the tongue has the most bacteria, so it is important to clean there gently.

## How often should you clean your tongue?

Clean your tongue once a day, preferably in the morning. Morning is best because bacteria accumulate overnight. You can also clean it at night before bed.

## Should you clean before or after brushing?

Clean your tongue after brushing. This removes any remaining bacteria and gives a fresher feeling.

## Common mistakes

- Scraping too hard and injuring the tongue
- Not cleaning the back of the tongue
- Using a dirty scraper
- Sharing tongue scrapers
- Not replacing plastic scrapers regularly
- Using a metal scraper with rough edges
- Cleaning only once and thinking the job is done
- Brushing the tongue instead of scraping (less effective)

## Tongue cleaning for children

Children can start tongue cleaning when they are old enough to follow instructions. Use a soft, child-sized tongue scraper and supervise them.

## Signs of tongue problems

See a dentist if you notice:

- Persistent white coating that does not clean off
- Red, swollen, or painful tongue
- Sores or ulcers that do not heal
- Sudden changes in tongue colour or texture
- Painful burning sensation

## Tongue cleaning and bad breath

Studies show that tongue cleaning can reduce bad breath by up to 50%. Combined with brushing and flossing, it is one of the most effective ways to maintain fresh breath.

## Tongue coating types and what they indicate

| Coating appearance | Possible cause | What to do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| White thin coating | Normal bacteria buildup | Regular tongue cleaning |
| White thick coating | Oral thrush or poor hygiene | See dentist if persistent |
| Yellow coating | Bacteria, acid reflux, or smoking | Clean tongue, address cause |
| Brown or black | Chromogenic bacteria, smoking, or medication | See dentist for evaluation |
| Geographic (red patches) | Geographic tongue condition | See dentist for diagnosis |
| Green coating | Fungal infection or poor hygiene | See dentist for treatment |

Monitoring your tongue's appearance can provide early warning signs of oral and systemic health issues.

## Frequently asked questions

**Should I clean my tongue every day?**
Yes, cleaning your tongue once a day helps reduce bad breath and bacteria.

**Is a tongue scraper better than a toothbrush?**
Yes, a tongue scraper is more effective at removing the tongue coating.

**Can tongue cleaning cause bleeding?**
If done gently, no. If your tongue bleeds, you may be scraping too hard.

**Does tongue cleaning improve bad breath?**
Yes, tongue cleaning can significantly reduce bad breath by removing odour-causing bacteria.

**Why is my tongue white?**
A white tongue is usually caused by a coating of bacteria, dead cells, and food debris. Regular cleaning helps. However, if the white coating persists despite cleaning, it could indicate oral thrush or other conditions and should be checked by a dentist.

**Can tongue cleaning improve my sense of taste?**
Yes, many people notice improved taste sensation within days of starting tongue cleaning. The coating on the tongue blocks taste receptors, and removing it allows flavours to come through more clearly.

**Should children use tongue scrapers?**
Children can start tongue cleaning from around age 5 or when they are old enough to follow instructions. Use a soft, child-sized scraper and supervise them to ensure they do not scrape too hard.

**Can I use mouthwash instead of tongue cleaning?**
Mouthwash reduces bacteria but does not physically remove the coating. Tongue cleaning and mouthwash work best together as part of a complete oral hygiene routine.

**How do I clean my tongue scraper?**
Rinse it thoroughly with water after each use. Once a week, wash it with warm water and soap or soak it in an antibacterial solution. Replace plastic scrapers every 3 to 6 months.

**Can tongue cleaning help with oral thrush?**
Tongue cleaning alone cannot treat oral thrush, which is a fungal infection requiring medication. However, regular tongue cleaning helps prevent the conditions that allow thrush to develop. If you suspect oral thrush, see Dr. Dash for proper diagnosis and treatment.

**Is it normal to gag when cleaning my tongue?**
Gagging is common when starting tongue cleaning, especially when reaching the back of the tongue. Start by cleaning only the front portion and gradually work further back as you become more comfortable. Over time, the gag reflex decreases with practice.

**Can tongue cleaning help with a sore throat?**
Tongue cleaning removes bacteria that can contribute to throat irritation. While it is not a treatment for sore throat, maintaining good oral hygiene including tongue cleaning may help reduce the frequency of throat infections.

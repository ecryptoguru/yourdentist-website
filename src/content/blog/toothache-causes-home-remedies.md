## Common causes of toothache

### Tooth decay

Cavities expose the inner layers of the tooth, causing pain when you eat sweet, hot, or cold foods.

### Pulp infection

When decay reaches the inner pulp, it causes severe, throbbing pain. This often needs root canal treatment.

### Gum disease

Inflamed or infected gums can cause dull, aching pain around teeth.

### Tooth fracture

A cracked or chipped tooth can expose the nerve and cause sharp pain when biting.

### Abscess

A dental abscess is a pocket of infection at the root of a tooth. It causes severe pain, swelling, and fever.

### Sensitivity

Worn enamel or receding gums can cause brief, sharp pain with hot, cold, or sweet stimuli.

### Impacted wisdom tooth

A wisdom tooth that does not have enough space to erupt can cause pain, swelling, and infection.

### Grinding or clenching

Bruxism can wear down teeth and cause jaw pain and toothache.

### Sinus infection

Sinus pressure can cause pain in the upper back teeth.

## Home remedies for temporary toothache relief

Home remedies can help until you see a dentist. They do not cure the underlying problem.

### Rinse with warm salt water

Mix half a teaspoon of salt in warm water and rinse for 30 seconds. This reduces inflammation and cleans the area.

### Cold compress

Apply a cold compress to the cheek for 15 minutes to reduce swelling and numb pain.

### Over-the-counter pain relief

Medicines like paracetamol or ibuprofen can reduce pain and swelling. Follow the package instructions.

### Clove oil

Applying a small amount of clove oil to the painful tooth can provide temporary numbing. Do not use on children.

### Avoid extreme temperatures

Avoid very hot, cold, sweet, or acidic foods and drinks if they trigger pain.

### Keep your head elevated

Elevating your head while sleeping can reduce blood flow to the area and lessen throbbing pain.

## What NOT to do for toothache

- Do not apply heat to a swollen face
- Do not place aspirin directly on the gum or tooth
- Do not use sharp objects to remove food stuck between teeth
- Do not ignore severe pain
- Do not delay treatment hoping it will go away

## When to see a dentist for toothache

See Dr. Arpita Dash immediately if you have:

- Severe pain lasting more than a day or two
- Swelling in the face or gums
- Fever with tooth pain
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing
- Pus or foul taste in the mouth
- Pain that worsens when lying down
- A knocked-out or broken tooth

## Preventing toothache

- Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
- Floss daily
- Visit the dentist every 6 months
- Do not ignore minor sensitivity
- Wear a mouthguard during sports
- Avoid chewing hard objects
- Treat cavities early before they reach the nerve
- Wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth
- Limit sugary and acidic foods

## Frequently asked questions

**What is the fastest way to relieve toothache?**
Rinse with warm salt water, take pain relievers, and apply a cold compress. See a dentist as soon as possible.

**Can toothache go away on its own?**
Pain may decrease temporarily, but the underlying problem usually worsens without treatment.

**When is toothache an emergency?**
Severe pain with swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing is a dental emergency.

**How much does toothache treatment cost?**
Costs range from ₹500 for extraction to ₹10,000 for root canal treatment.

**Can I use clove oil for toothache?**
Clove oil can provide temporary relief, but it does not treat the cause. See a dentist for proper treatment.

**Why does my toothache get worse at night?**
When you lie down, blood flow to the head increases, which can increase pressure and pain in an inflamed tooth. Elevating your head with pillows can help reduce throbbing pain.

**Can a sinus infection cause toothache?**
Yes, sinus pressure can cause pain in the upper back teeth. If you have a cold or sinus congestion along with toothache, the sinuses may be the cause.

**How long can I wait with a toothache before seeing a dentist?**
Mild sensitivity can wait a few days, but severe or worsening pain should be seen within 24 to 48 hours. Pain with swelling or fever is an emergency.

**Can stress cause toothache?**
Stress can cause teeth grinding (bruxism), which leads to tooth wear, sensitivity, and jaw pain. A nightguard can protect your teeth.

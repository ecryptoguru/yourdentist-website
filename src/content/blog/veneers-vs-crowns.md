Veneers and crowns are two of the most popular cosmetic dental treatments. Both can transform your smile, but they serve different purposes. ## What are veneers?

Veneers are thin shells of porcelain or composite resin bonded to the front surface of teeth. They improve the colour, shape, and alignment of visible teeth. Veneers require minimal tooth reduction, usually only 0.5 to 1 mm of the front surface.

## What are crowns?

Crowns are caps that cover the entire tooth. They restore strength, shape, and appearance. Crowns require more tooth reduction than veneers, typically 1.5 to 2 mm around all sides of the tooth.

## When veneers are the better choice

Veneers are ideal when your teeth are healthy but you want to improve:

- Discoloured teeth that do not respond to whitening
- Slightly chipped or worn teeth
- Minor gaps between front teeth
- Slightly misaligned or uneven teeth
- Irregularly shaped teeth

Veneers work best for front teeth that have healthy enamel and no large fillings.

## When crowns are the better choice

Crowns are ideal when teeth need structural support:

- Teeth with large fillings or extensive decay
- Teeth that have had root canal treatment
- Cracked or fractured teeth
- Severely worn teeth
- Back teeth that need strength for chewing
- Teeth that are too damaged for veneers

## The veneer procedure

1. **Consultation:** Dr. Dash examines your teeth and discusses goals.
2. **Preparation:** A thin layer of enamel is removed from the front surface.
3. **Impression:** A digital or physical impression is taken.
4. **Temporary veneers:** Temporary veneers may be placed while the permanent ones are made.
5. **Bonding:** The permanent veneers are bonded to the teeth with dental adhesive.
6. **Follow-up:** A check visit to ensure proper fit and bite.

The process takes 2 to 3 visits over 1 to 2 weeks.

## The crown procedure

1. **Consultation:** Dr. Dash examines the tooth and takes X-rays.
2. **Preparation:** The tooth is reshaped to make room for the crown.
3. **Impression:** A digital or physical impression is taken.
4. **Temporary crown:** A temporary crown is placed while the permanent one is made.
5. **Cementation:** The permanent crown is cemented onto the tooth.
6. **Follow-up:** A check visit to ensure proper fit and bite.

The process takes 2 to 3 visits over 1 to 2 weeks.

## Can you get veneers and crowns together?

Yes. Some patients need crowns on damaged teeth and veneers on healthy front teeth. Dr. Dash plans combined treatments to ensure the colour and shape match across all restorations.

## Veneers and crowns for front vs back teeth

| Tooth position | Recommended treatment | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Upper front teeth | Veneers or crowns | Veneers if healthy, crowns if damaged |
| Lower front teeth | Veneers or crowns | Veneers if healthy, crowns if damaged |
| Canines | Usually crowns | Higher biting force |
| Premolars | Crowns | Chewing force |
| Molars | Crowns | Maximum chewing force |

Front teeth are the primary candidates for veneers because they are visible and experience less biting force. Back teeth almost always need crowns for structural strength.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are veneers better than crowns?**
It depends on your teeth. Veneers are better for healthy teeth that need cosmetic improvement. Crowns are better for damaged or weakened teeth.

**How long do veneers last?**
Porcelain veneers last 10 to 15 years with proper care. Composite veneers last 5 to 7 years.

**Do veneers damage teeth?**
Veneers require removing a thin layer of enamel, which is irreversible. However, the amount removed is minimal compared to crowns.

**Can I whiten veneers or crowns?**
No. Veneers and crowns do not respond to whitening. If they become stained, they need to be replaced.

**How much do veneers cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Porcelain veneers cost ₹10,000 to ₹20,000 per tooth at YourDentist.

**How much do crowns cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Crowns cost ₹4,000 to ₹18,000 per tooth depending on the material. Zirconia crowns are the most popular for their durability and natural appearance.

**Can I get veneers after braces?**
Yes, many patients get veneers after braces to perfect the shape and colour of their teeth. Dr. Dash recommends waiting at least 1 month after braces removal before placing veneers.

**Do veneers and crowns look natural?**
Yes, modern porcelain and zirconia restorations are highly aesthetic and can be colour-matched to blend seamlessly with your natural teeth. Dr. Dash uses custom shade matching for the best results.

**What happens if a veneer or crown breaks?**
A broken veneer usually needs replacement. A broken crown can sometimes be repaired but often needs replacement. Avoid biting hard objects to prevent damage to both veneers and crowns.

## What are wisdom teeth?

Wisdom teeth, also called third molars, are located at the back of the mouth. There are usually four wisdom teeth, one in each corner. Some people have fewer or none at all.

## When do wisdom teeth cause problems?

Wisdom teeth can cause problems when:

- There is not enough space for them to erupt
- They grow at an angle or sideways
- They are impacted (stuck under gum or bone)
- They only partially emerge
- They crowd or damage nearby teeth
- They trap food and bacteria

## Signs you need wisdom tooth extraction

- Pain or swelling at the back of the mouth
- Difficulty opening the mouth
- Bad taste or smell near the wisdom tooth
- Gum infection or abscess
- Crowding of front teeth
- Damage to adjacent molars
- Recurring soreness in the jaw

## Wisdom tooth extraction procedure

### Step 1: Examination and X-rays

Dr. Dash examines the area and takes X-rays or a CBCT scan to see the position of the wisdom tooth and its roots.

### Step 2: Anaesthesia

Local anaesthesia numbs the area. For complex cases or anxious patients, sedation may be discussed.

### Step 3: Incision and bone removal

If the tooth is impacted, a small incision is made in the gum, and some bone may be removed to access the tooth.

### Step 4: Tooth removal

The tooth may be divided into pieces for easier removal. The socket is then cleaned.

### Step 5: Stitches

Gum tissue may be stitched closed. Some stitches dissolve on their own; others are removed after a week.

### Step 6: Gauze placement

Gauze is placed over the socket to control bleeding. Pressure is applied for 30 to 45 minutes.

## Recovery after wisdom tooth extraction

| Time | What to expect |
| --- | --- |
| First 24 hours | Bleeding, swelling, and mild to moderate pain |
| 2 to 3 days | Swelling peaks, then starts to reduce |
| 1 week | Stitches removed, most discomfort gone |
| 2 weeks | Gum tissue mostly healed |

## Aftercare tips

- Bite on gauze for 30 to 45 minutes after surgery
- Apply ice packs for the first 24 hours
- Take prescribed pain medication and antibiotics
- Eat soft, cool foods for the first few days
- Avoid hot, spicy, and crunchy foods
- Do not use a straw for 24 hours
- Do not spit or rinse vigorously for 24 hours
- Avoid smoking for at least 48 hours
- Sleep with your head elevated
- Gently rinse with warm salt water after 24 hours

## Risks of wisdom tooth extraction

Wisdom tooth extraction is generally safe. Possible complications include:

- Dry socket (blood clot dislodges, exposing bone)
- Infection
- Nerve injury (usually temporary)
- Swelling and bruising
- Bleeding

Dr. Dash provides detailed aftercare to minimise these risks.

## Types of wisdom tooth impaction

| Type | Description | Difficulty of extraction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fully erupted | Tooth is fully visible | Easy |
| Soft tissue impaction | Covered by gum | Moderate |
| Partial bony impaction | Partly in jawbone | Difficult |
| Full bony impaction | Completely in jawbone | Most difficult |
| Horizontal impaction | Tooth lies sideways | Very difficult |
| Mesial impaction | Tooth angled toward front | Moderate to difficult |

The type of impaction affects the extraction method, recovery time, and cost.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is wisdom tooth extraction painful?**
The procedure is done under anaesthesia, so you should not feel pain. Mild discomfort after surgery is normal.

**How long does wisdom tooth extraction take?**
A simple extraction takes 20 to 40 minutes. Complex or impacted teeth may take longer.

**How much does wisdom tooth extraction cost in Bhubaneswar?**
Wisdom tooth extraction costs ₹1,000 to ₹12,000 depending on complexity.

**When can I eat normally after extraction?**
Soft foods for 2 to 3 days. Gradually return to normal diet after 1 week.

**What is dry socket?**
Dry socket happens when the blood clot dislodges from the socket, exposing bone. It causes severe pain and needs treatment.

**Can all four wisdom teeth be removed at once?**
Yes, all four can be removed in one appointment under sedation. However, some patients prefer to do one side at a time for easier recovery and eating.

**How do I prevent dry socket?**
Do not use straws, do not spit forcefully, avoid smoking, and follow all aftercare instructions. These actions can dislodge the blood clot.

**When can I return to work after wisdom tooth extraction?
Most people return to work after 2 to 3 days. For complex surgical extractions, 3 to 5 days may be needed.

**Do all wisdom teeth need to be removed?**
No. If wisdom teeth are fully erupted, positioned correctly, and easy to clean, they may not need extraction. Dr. Dash will evaluate each tooth individually.

## What are metal-ceramic crowns?

Metal-ceramic crowns, also called porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM) crowns, have a metal core covered by a ceramic outer layer. They have been used for decades and are strong and affordable.

The metal core provides strength, while the ceramic outer layer provides a tooth-like appearance. However, over time, the gumline can recede and expose a dark metal line. This is the main aesthetic drawback of PFM crowns.

## What are zirconia crowns?

Zirconia crowns are made from zirconium oxide, a strong, white, biocompatible ceramic. They are metal-free, highly durable, and look very natural.

Zirconia is sometimes called "ceramic steel" because of its exceptional strength. It can withstand the forces of chewing and grinding, making it suitable for both front and back teeth. Unlike PFM crowns, zirconia crowns never show a dark metal line at the gumline.

## When zirconia crowns are better

Zirconia crowns are the best choice when:

- You want the most natural appearance
- You have a metal allergy
- The crown is for a front tooth
- You need a strong crown for a back tooth
- You want a long-lasting restoration
- You are concerned about the dark metal line of PFM crowns

## When metal-ceramic crowns are better

Metal-ceramic crowns are a good choice when:

- Cost is a concern
- The crown is for a back tooth
- Strength is more important than appearance
- Aesthetic demands are moderate

## The crown procedure

1. **Consultation:** Dr. Dash examines the tooth and recommends a crown material.
2. **Tooth preparation:** The tooth is reshaped to make room for the crown.
3. **Impression:** A digital or physical impression is taken.
4. **Temporary crown:** A temporary crown is placed.
5. **Crown fabrication:** The permanent crown is made in a dental lab.
6. **Cementation:** The permanent crown is cemented onto the tooth.
7. **Follow-up:** Bite and fit are checked.

The process takes 1 to 2 weeks.

## Zirconia crowns after root canal treatment

Teeth that have had root canal treatment become brittle over time. They often need crowns to prevent fracture. Zirconia crowns are an excellent choice because they are strong and natural-looking. Most patients choose zirconia for front teeth and monolithic zirconia for back teeth.

## Caring for your crowns

Whether you choose zirconia or metal-ceramic, proper care extends the life of your crown:

- Brush twice daily with a soft-bristle toothbrush
- Floss daily around the crown
- Avoid biting very hard objects like ice, nuts, or pens
- Wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth
- Visit the dentist every 6 months for checkups
- Avoid using teeth as tools to open packages

## Frequently asked questions

**Which is better: zirconia or metal-ceramic crowns?**
Zirconia is better for appearance and biocompatibility. Metal-ceramic is a good budget option for back teeth.

**How long do zirconia crowns last?**
Zirconia crowns can last 12 to 15 years or more with proper care.

**Can zirconia crowns break?**
Zirconia is very strong, but like natural teeth, it can fracture under extreme force. Avoid biting very hard objects.

**Do zirconia crowns look natural?**
Yes, especially layered and high-translucency zirconia crowns. They mimic the light-transmitting properties of natural teeth.

**Are metal-ceramic crowns safe?**
Yes, metal-ceramic crowns are safe. However, the metal core can sometimes show a dark line at the gumline over time.

**Can I replace my old metal-ceramic crowns with zirconia?**
Yes, old PFM crowns with visible metal lines can be replaced with zirconia crowns for better aesthetics. Dr. Dash will assess the tooth underneath before replacement.

**How long does the crown procedure take?**
The entire process takes 1 to 2 weeks, typically requiring two visits. The first visit involves tooth preparation and impressions, and the second visit is for crown cementation.

**Do crowns require special toothpaste?**
No special toothpaste is needed. Use regular fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristle brush. Avoid abrasive whitening toothpastes that can scratch the crown surface.

**Can I get a crown on the same day?**
Same-day crowns are possible with CAD/CAM technology, but YourDentist typically uses lab-fabricated crowns for superior fit and aesthetics. The process takes 1 to 2 weeks, during which you wear a temporary crown.

**Will my crown match my other teeth?**
Yes, Dr. Dash uses custom shade matching to ensure your crown blends with your natural teeth. The dental lab receives detailed shade instructions and photographs to create a crown that matches your tooth colour, translucency, and surface texture.

**What happens if my crown falls off?**
If a crown falls off, keep it safe and call YourDentist immediately. Do not try to glue it back yourself. Dr. Dash can recement it if the tooth underneath is intact. If the tooth is damaged, additional treatment may be needed before recementation.
